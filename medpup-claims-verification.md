## CLAIM 2: Bahamas Pet Import Permit - "Click2Clear system - $11.20, 1-3 business days"

### Status: CONTRADICTED

#### What the Documents Actually Say
The MedPup business plan claims: "Click2Clear system - $11.20, 1-3 business days" for getting a pet import permit to the Bahamas.

#### What PetTravel.com Says (Updated Feb 9, 2026)
Source: https://www.pettravel.com/information/pet-passports/bahamas-pet-import-requirements/

**QUOTED TEXT (from page):**
> "The Commonwealth of the Bahamas requires that importers obtain an import permit prior to entry. The issuance of the permit can take 6-8 weeks without expedited services."

**Key requirements found on this page:**
1. **Import Permit Required** - YES, confirmed
2. **Processing Time** - "6-8 weeks without expedited services" vs claimed "1-3 business days" — **CONTRADICTED**
3. **Cost** - Not specified on PetTravel.com; the "$11.20" figure is **UNVERIFIED**
4. **Click2Clear** - **NO MENTION** of any "Click2Clear system" on the Bahamas pet import requirements page. This term may refer to a different country's customs system or be fabricated.

#### Actual Permit Application Requirements (from PetTravel.com):
- **Form**: IMP1(B) form mentioned for sea entry: "Pets entering the Bahamas by sea should have an import permit, IMP1(B) form, proof of rabies vaccination, and a health certificate available for customs agents."
- **Rabies vaccine**: Required between 30 days and 10 months before entry for primary vaccinations
- **Microchip**: Required (15-digit)
- **Health Certificate**: Must be issued within 48 hours of entry
- **Minimum age**: 6 months
- **Breed restrictions**: Pit Bulls, Presa Canario, Cane Corso, American Bulldog, and Staffordshire Terrier prohibited
- **Quarantine**: None if regulations met; otherwise "quarantine, returned, or euthanized"

#### Official Bahamas Government Site
- The Bahamas Agriculture/Veterinary Services web portal (https://www.bahamas.gov.bs/wps/portal/public/Agriculture%20and%20Fisheries/Agriculture/Agriculture/) returns 403 Forbidden from this server.
- No actual permit application form could be retrieved.

#### VERDICT: CONTRADICTED. 
The "6-8 weeks" processing time from PetTravel.com directly contradicts the "1-3 business days" claim. The "Click2Clear" system is not mentioned in any pet import documentation found. The "$11.20" fee is unverified.

## CLAIM 3: Port Everglades CBP Pet Re-Entry

### Status: PARTIALLY VERIFIED (General CBP Information)

#### Key Findings
- **CDC Page Found**: "Bringing a Dog into the U.S." at https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/index.html — but no specific Port Everglades information.
- **CBP Pet Page**: CBP's general pet page at /travel/pets returns "Requested page not found" (404). The page at /travel/us-citizens/pets also 404s.
- **CBP has no specific "Port Everglades pet inspection" page** accessible.

#### What is Known
- Pets returning to the US generally need:
  - CDC Dog Import Form (if coming from a country with dog rabies risk)
  - Proof of rabies vaccination (US-origin dogs returning typically need current US rabies certificate)
  - Health certificate (may be required depending on circumstances)
  - CBP agricultural inspection for animal products

#### Port Everglades Specifics
- **NO specific information** found about pet inspection procedures at Port Everglades (cruise/ferry port in Fort Lauderdale).
- General cruise port pet re-entry: dogs are typically processed by CBP along with their owners, with agricultural specialists available for pet checks.
- The key issue is that **most commercial ferries from the Bahamas arrive at either Port Everglades or Port of Palm Beach**, and pets arriving by sea are processed at the CBP inspection area.

#### VERDICT: UNVERIFIED for specific Port Everglades procedures. 
General CBP pet re-entry rules exist but no specific Port Everglades guidance was found.

## CLAIM 4: Pet Travel to Bahamas from Florida by Ferry

### Status: PARTIALLY VERIFIED (Requirements Known, No Specific Ferry Experience Found)

#### Ferry Pet Travel Requirements (from PetTravel.com)
**QUOTED TEXT:**
> "Pets entering the Bahamas by sea should have an import permit, IMP1(B) form, proof of rabies vaccination, and a health certificate available for customs agents. Customs is available at major ports."

> "Importers should inform veterinary officials in advance, as they will need to be available to check your pet and its pet passport documentation after landing."

#### Requirements Summary for Ferry Travel:
1. **Import Permit** (IMP1(B) form) — apply well in advance (6-8 weeks per PetTravel.com)
2. **Rabies Vaccination** — current, administered between 30 days and 10 months prior
3. **Microchip** — 15-digit
4. **Health Certificate** — issued within 48 hours of travel
5. **Veterinary exam in Bahamas** — within 48 hours of arrival
6. **Dog license** — within 48 hours of entry if staying longer than 6 days
7. **Customs duty** — payable at point of entry

#### Specific Ferry Experience Accounts
- **TripsWithPets.com**: Site is under maintenance — no content available.
- **BringFido.com/bahamas**: Returns 404.
- **No first-hand accounts found** of people bringing pets from Florida to the Bahamas by ferry. This is likely because the major ferries (Bahamas Express, Balearia, etc.) generally do not allow pets on passenger decks, and private boat travel with pets is more common but less documented online.

#### VERDICT: PARTIALLY VERIFIED.
The paperwork requirements for sea entry are reasonably documented by PetTravel.com. However, NO specific verified accounts of ferry travel with pets from Florida to the Bahamas were found. The lack of such accounts may indicate that:
- Commercial ferries may not permit pets
- Private/charter boat travel is the typical method
- The business plan's assumption that pets can freely travel by ferry may be incorrect
