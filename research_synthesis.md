## 2. Full Data Analysis: Puerto Rico

### The Claim vs. Reality

**Claim:** "Puerto Rico veterinary services are 40-60% cheaper than mainland US."
**Reality:** ~13.7% average savings on standard commercial care (per Banfield published pricing).

### Published Price Data (Banfield Pet Hospital — Standardized Corporate Pricing)

| Service | Florida Metro | Puerto Rico (Bayamón/San Juan) | Variance |
|---------|--------------|-------------------------------|----------|
| Office visit & exam | $79.50 | $54.95 | **-30.88%** |
| Rabies vaccination | $32.00 | $24.24 | **-24.25%** |
| Dental cleaning | $425.00 | $334.95 | **-21.19%** |
| Canine neuter (<6 mo) | $350.00 | $375.95 | **+7.41% MORE** |
| Feline spay (<6 mo) | $400.00 | $400.95 | **+0.24% IDENTICAL** |
| Mean variance | | | **-13.73%** |

**Why Banfield data matters:** Banfield uses a standardized medical pricing index across all locations. This isolates the territory cost difference from clinic-to-clinic variation. It's the cleanest comparison available.

### Why PR Can't Hit 40-60% Savings

| Structural Factor | Impact |
|------------------|--------|
| Jones Act shipping | All vet equipment, drugs, supplies shipped via US-flagged vessels = inflated wholesale costs |
| LUMA Energy utility rates | Commercial electricity ~2x mainland average |
| Vet shortage | Only 200-300 vets on entire island for 3M+ people; no PR veterinary school; talent commands mainland-competitive wages |
| IVU sales tax (11.5%) | Applied to ALL veterinary services — directly erodes savings |
| Nonprofit/subsidized confusion | $25 spay clinics for strays ("satos") — travelers mistake humanitarian prices for commercial rates |

### Critical Friction: The Screwworm Requirement (Nov 2024)

As of November 22, 2024, Mexico is considered screwworm-affected. ALL dogs (including US-origin) returning to the US must have:
1. **Screwworm freedom certification** by a SENASICA-authorized Mexican vet within 5 days of return
2. **Inspection at US port of entry**
3. **CDC Dog Import Form** (free, online, ~5 minutes)
4. Proof of valid **rabies vaccination**

**Cost:** Approximately $30-80 for the screwworm examination and certificate.
**Friction level:** Moderate — requires coordination but MexiVet handles it at scale.

## 6. Consumer Willingness to Travel

### Key Data Points from Research

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Urban/suburban owners' travel radius | **2-6 miles** | JAVMA |
| Owners skipping care >10km away | **54%** vs 46% <10km | Gallup |
| Owners reporting cost as barrier | **71%** (7 in 10) | Gallup 2025 |
| Were NOT offered cheaper alternatives | **73%** | Gallup 2025 |
| Would double budget with payment plans | **64%** | Gallup 2025 |
| Cross-border pet travel surge | **68% increase** | Yahoo News |
| Annual Mexico pet crossings | **33,500+** | Yahoo News |

### The Equation That Defines the Market

```
Willingness to Travel = (Net Savings × Pet Emotional Value) / (Logistical Hassle × Pet Stress)
```

**What this means for each model:**
- **Georgia drive (10-21% savings):** Owner must drive 14 hours round-trip. The equation FAILS for almost everyone.
- **Puerto Rico air (13.7% savings):** Owner must fly + hotel + 2.5h flight. The equation FAILS.
- **Texas land border (76.8% savings):** Even with complexity, the savings are so large the equation WORKS — IF someone handles the logistics.

**The Concierge Solution:** By removing the "Logistical Hassle" and "Owner Travel Time" variables from the equation (owner drops pet at local hub, company handles transport), the model succeeds because it transforms a logistical nightmare into a localized drop-off. MexiVet Express proves this: they handle everything, the owner just delivers the pet to a San Diego location and picks it up the same day.

### The Cross-Border Model is Verified But Has Geographic Constraints

The Texas land border is the ONLY model with verified 70-80% savings — high enough to support a concierge transport fee and still deliver real savings to clients. But it requires either:
- Relocating to Texas (El Paso, Brownsville, or McAllen area)
- Hiring a Texas-based point person
- Running the operation remotely with a local coordinator

### The Florida-Based Alternative is Stronger Than Any Travel Model

The research on consumer willingness proves that FL pet owners will NOT drive 6 hours to Georgia or fly to Puerto Rico for 10-20% savings. They WILL hand their pet to a trusted concierge if savings are 50-80% — but that requires the Texas border.

**The realization:** Maybe the best Florida-based model isn't travel at all. A low-cost surgical center (Dr. Kelly's model) or membership-based primary care network (Small Door model) addresses the cost crisis without any travel friction, without border complications, and without a 14-hour drive.

Both are proven, scalable, and directly address the **71% of owners citing cost as the reason they skip care** — and the **73% who were never offered a cheaper alternative.**
