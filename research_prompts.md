## SECTION 1: FULL CONTEXT FOR THE RESEARCH AGENT

### What This Project Is

This is a business concept exploration for a Florida-based operator who wants to address the US veterinary cost crisis — veterinary costs rose 47% since 2020 (BLS confirmed), 50%+ of pet owners have forgone care due to cost (Gallup/PetSmart Charities), and pet insurance penetration is only 5% vs 25-35% in comparable markets. There is clearly high demand for lower-cost pet care.

### What's Been Tried So Far

**Original concept (MedPup):** A concierge service bringing US pet owners from Florida to Freeport, Bahamas by ferry for same-day veterinary procedures. After extensive research:

- **Ferry confirmed:** Balearia Caribbean accepts pets in kennels, $150/leg ($300 RT), 72hr advance booking
- **Bahamas import confirmed:** BAHFSA permit $50, 24-48h processing, health certificate 10-day window
- **Freeport vet clinics identified:** 4 clinics exist, but NONE publicly list orthopedic surgical capability — only general "Surgery." Dental partially confirmed (February special). No CareCredit. No functional website.
- **Cost analysis showed margins too thin:** $300 ferry + $50 permit + $100-300 vet exam + $100-200 hotel = $600-1,050/pet. At $900 concierge fee: 17% margin best case, loss in worst case.

**Cancún expansion researched:** 15+ clinics identified. VetCancun and CAP Cancún confirmed surgery+dental+orthopedic+travel certificates. But:
- Air transport: $220-500 RT per pet
- Mexico import: health certificate + APHIS (+$38-50) + screwworm certificate (+$30-80)
- Total: $645-1,975 per pet. At $900 fee: margins too thin except for high-end orthopedic cases.
- Air cost kills the savings — same problem as Freeport ferry.

**Conclusion on original model:** Air/ferry-based pet medical tourism has margins too thin at the $900 concierge fee. Only works for high-complexity procedures (orthopedic at $2,000+ fee) but that's a niche.

### Three New Opportunities Identified

Through further research, three alternative models emerged:

1. **Puerto Rico Air (Florida to San Juan):** US territory, zero customs/APHIS, direct flights 2.5h from FLL/MIA, estimated 40-60% lower vet costs. Zero competition.

2. **Texas Land Border (El Paso-Juárez, etc.):** Proven model — MexiVet Express does 80 pets/week at SD-Tijuana. Documented 70-80% savings on procedures. No organized platform exists for Texas border. Best margins but not Florida-based.

3. **Georgia Drive (South Florida to South Georgia):** Simplest — drive 6h, zero customs, zero paperwork. But savings may be smaller than estimated (see below).

### CRITICAL: The Georgia Lesson (Very Important)

**Initial assumption:** "Georgia is 30-50% cheaper than Florida."
**Reality (from verified market data, provided by user):** Georgia is approximately **10% cheaper than Florida statewide.** Only extreme Miami-vs-rural-South-Georgia comparisons hit 30-40% on specific procedures. The formula:

- Avg FL office visit: $69.60
- Avg GA office visit: $62.60
- Actual savings: (69.60 - 62.60) / 69.60 = **10.06%**

**Lesson:** Never assume savings percentages without verified market data. The 40-60% estimate for Puerto Rico and the 70-80% claims for Mexico border ALSO need to be verified the same way — with actual market data, not assumptions or anecdotes.

### What We NEED From This Research (The Key Questions)

The business concept feasibility hinges on these unverified questions:

1. **Is Puerto Rico actually cheaper for vet care, and by how much?** If PR is only 10% cheaper (like Georgia), the model doesn't work — airfare costs eat the savings. If PR is 30-50% cheaper, it's viable. Need real data.

2. **Are Texas land border pricing claims (70-80% savings) actually real and verifiable?** This is the only model where data so far suggests high enough savings to work. Need to verify with actual price lists.

3. **What business models in pet cost crisis actually exist and scale?** Beyond travel-based arbitrage, are there subscription, telehealth, or insurance-adjacent models that work?

4. **Would Florida pet owners actually participate?** Even if savings are real, will owners drive 6h or fly for cheaper vet care? Is there demand?

## PROMPT 1: Puerto Rico Veterinary Pricing — Real Market Data

**Goal:** Find actual veterinary pricing data for Puerto Rico (specifically San Juan metro area) for dogs. Determine if the "40-60% below mainland" claim is real or a myth (like the Georgia 30-50% myth).

**Business context:** If PR is only 10-15% cheaper (like Georgia), the model barely works. If 30%+, it's viable. This is the single most important question for the Puerto Rico opportunity.

**Methodology:**
1. Search for published veterinary pricing data for Puerto Rico — MarketWatch Guides 2026 state-by-state data (DOES IT INCLUDE TERRITORIES?)
2. Search for "average vet office visit cost Puerto Rico 2025 2026" "veterinary pricing San Juan PR"
3. Search in Spanish: "cuanto cuesta una consulta veterinaria en Puerto Rico" "precios veterinaria San Juan PR 2025"
4. Look for pet insurance premium data by state (Spot Pet, Lemonade, Nationwide often publish state-by-state claim averages — PR may be listed)
5. Search for cost-of-living comparisons: "Puerto Rico cost of living vs mainland healthcare" "veterinary services cost index PR"
6. Search for actual clinic pricing: "Hospital Veterinario San Juan PR price list" "Veterinary price list Puerto Rico"
7. Look for AVMA or PR veterinary board data on practice revenues or fee surveys
8. Check if any vet cost calculators (like petcarecostcalculator.com) include PR

**Specifically find pricing for:**
- Baseline office visit / exam fee (most important benchmark)
- Spay (female dog)
- Neuter (male dog)
- Dental cleaning (under anesthesia)
- Mass/tumor removal
- X-ray (single view)

**For each procedure, find:**
- The price in PR
- The comparable price in Florida (from MarketWatch or similar)
- Calculate: (FL_price - PR_price) / FL_price × 100 = savings percentage
- Source URL and date

**Deliverable:**
- A comparison table: Procedure | FL Price (source) | PR Price (source) | Savings %
- Show your work — the formula for each calculation
- Source URLs for every data point
- Conclusion: Is PR's cost advantage real or a myth? What's the actual gap?
