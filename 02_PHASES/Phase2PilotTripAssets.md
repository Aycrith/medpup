# MEDPUP POST‑OPERATIVE CARE INSTRUCTIONS

**Pet Name:** [Pet Name]  
**Procedure:** [Procedure]  
**Date of Surgery:** [Date]  
**Surgeon:** [Name], DVM | **Clinic:** [Clinic Name], Freeport  
**Surgical Report Attached:** Yes

### IMMEDIATE CARE (First 24 Hours)

Your dog has undergone general anesthesia and surgery. For the next 24 hours:
- Keep your dog in a warm, quiet, confined area (small room or crate).
- Offer a small amount of water only. If no vomiting after 2 hours, offer a small meal (half the normal portion).
- Expect grogginess, whining, or disorientation – this is normal after anesthesia.
- **NOT normal:** Pale gums, difficulty breathing, continuous bleeding, inability to stand, severe vomiting. Call the Clinic immediately and then contact MedPup.

### INCISION CARE
- **Do NOT** get the incision wet for 10‑14 days. No bathing, no swimming.
- **Do NOT** allow your dog to lick or chew the incision. Use the provided e‑collar at all times (except during supervised feeding).
- Check the incision daily. Slight redness/swelling is normal. **Call your vet if you see:** discharge (especially yellow/green), opening of the wound, foul odor, excessive swelling.

### ACTIVITY RESTRICTION
- Leash walks only for bathroom breaks for **7‑10 days** (or as specified below).
- No running, jumping, stair climbing, or rough play.
- Confine when unsupervised to prevent sudden movements.

### Procedure‑Specific Addendums (Attach as Needed)

#### Dental Surgery Addendum
```markdown
### ADDITIONAL INSTRUCTIONS: DENTAL SURGERY

- **Diet:** Feed only soft food (canned/pâté or soaked kibble) for 5‑7 days.
- **Pain:** Some drool tinged with blood is normal for 24‑48 hours.
- **Chewing:** No hard chews, toys, or bones for 14 days.
- **Sutures:** If absorbable sutures were placed in the mouth, they will dissolve on their own in 2‑4 weeks. Do not pull at them.
- **Bad Breath:** Temporary odor is normal as the mouth heals. If it worsens after Day 3, contact your vet.
```

- **Bandage:** Keep the bandage clean and dry. [If applicable: Change bandage every X days per Clinic instructions.]
- **Activity:** Strict rest for 10‑14 days to prevent tension on the incision.
- **Suture Removal:** Non‑absorbable sutures/staples must be removed by your primary vet in 10‑14 days.

#### Spay/Neuter Addendum (Large Breed)
```markdown
### ADDITIONAL INSTRUCTIONS: SPAY / NEUTER

- **E‑collar:** Must be worn at all times for 10‑14 days. No exceptions.
- **Activity:** Leash walks only. No off‑leash activity, dog parks, or daycare for 14 days.
- **Male dogs:** Scrotal swelling is common for 3‑5 days. Contact your vet if it increases after Day 5 or becomes painful.
- **Female dogs:** Monitor the incision closely. A small amount of serous (clear/light pink) discharge is normal. Contact your vet for any heavy bleeding or purulent discharge.
