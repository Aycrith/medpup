
# Phase 3: Veterinary Referral Outreach Campaign

## 1. The Strategy

General practice veterinarians are the single highest-converting referral source for surgical cases. When a vet diagnoses a dog with a torn ACL, BOAS, or advanced dental disease and the owner balks at the $3,000–$6,000 specialist estimate, that vet currently has no good answer. MedPup becomes their answer.

**Target:** 200 general practice clinics in Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, and Tampa-Orlando metros over 4 weeks.

**Goal:** Secure 20 signed Referral Partner Agreements, generating a steady stream of pre-qualified surgical leads.

## 3. Follow‑Up Phone Call Script (3 Days After Email)

**Goal:** Secure a 10‑minute call, or if the vet is interested immediately, schedule a video meeting.

### Opening
"Hi, this is [Your Name] with MedPup. I sent an email earlier this week about an affordable surgery option for your clients who can't afford specialist referrals. Did you have a chance to see it?"

**If no:** "No worries – the short version: we coordinate day‑trips to an accredited Bahamas clinic where TPLO is $1,200 instead of $5,000, and you stay the primary vet for follow‑up. Could I have 10 minutes to explain how it works and send you our partner clinic report?"

**If yes but hesitant:** "What's your biggest concern about referring a client overseas?"

### Handling Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm worried about quality of care." | "Fair concern. Our partner clinic is inspected by us in person, uses the WHO Surgical Safety Checklist, and the surgeon is board‑certified with 15+ years of experience. I'll send you the full inspection report – photos, credentials, equipment list. You can judge for yourself." |
| "What about liability?" | "You're not referring to us – you're simply informing the client of an option they can pursue. MedPup is a logistics service, not a veterinary practice. You have zero clinical liability for the overseas procedure. The client signs a waiver acknowledging you aren't responsible for outcomes abroad." |
| "How do I handle follow‑up if something goes wrong?" | "You receive a complete surgical report and direct surgeon contact before the patient returns. In our protocol, we ask the surgeon to be available for a handoff call with you. And we pay you for the post‑op recheck – it's a net positive for your practice." |
| "This sounds like medical tourism – is it ethical?" | "It is. The AVMA recognizes that international referral can be appropriate when U.S. costs create a barrier to necessary care. You're advocating for your patient's best interest. We don't pay kickbacks; we pay you for clinical work you actually perform." |
| "I don't want to lose the client." | "You won't. In fact, you'll retain a client who otherwise might euthanize or surrender their dog because they can't afford the specialist. They'll be grateful to you forever." |

### Closing
"Could we schedule a 15‑minute video call this week? I'll walk you through a case example, show you the clinic, and send a draft referral agreement. No commitment – just information."
