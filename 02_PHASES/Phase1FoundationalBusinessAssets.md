```
OPERATING AGREEMENT OF MEDPUP VETERINARY CONCIERGE, LLC

THIS OPERATING AGREEMENT is made as of [DATE], by and among the Members listed on Schedule A.

ARTICLE I: FORMATION
1.1 Name: MedPup Veterinary Concierge, LLC
1.2 State of Formation: Florida
1.3 Registered Agent: [NAME, ADDRESS]
1.4 Purpose: The Company is formed to provide non‑clinical management, logistical, and administrative services to veterinary practices, including but not limited to the coordination of international veterinary care travel packages.

ARTICLE II: MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE
2.1 The Company shall be manager‑managed.
2.2 The Manager shall be [NAME], who shall have full authority to bind the Company in all matters consistent with this Agreement.

ARTICLE III: LIMITATION ON CLINICAL PRACTICE
3.1 The Company shall not engage in the practice of veterinary medicine as defined by Florida Statute 474.202.
3.2 All clinical decisions, including diagnosis, treatment planning, surgical performance, and prescription of medications, shall remain the sole responsibility of the licensed veterinarian(s) providing such services.
3.3 The Company's role is strictly limited to: (a) marketing and client acquisition; (b) scheduling and travel logistics; (c) document preparation and regulatory compliance; (d) billing and collection for non‑clinical concierge services; and (e) facility and equipment management.

ARTICLE IV: RELATIONSHIP WITH PROFESSIONAL CORPORATION
4.1 The Company shall enter into a Management Services Agreement with a Florida Professional Corporation ("Friendly PC") owned entirely by one or more licensed veterinarians.
4.2 The Management Services Agreement shall provide for a fair market value management fee paid by the PC to the Company in exchange for the non‑clinical services listed in Article III.
4.3 Under no circumstances shall the Company: (a) employ veterinarians; (b) direct clinical decision‑making; (c) share in professional fees for veterinary services; or (d) hold itself out as a provider of veterinary medical services.

ARTICLE V: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
5.1 Each Member shall contribute the amount set forth on Schedule A.

ARTICLE VI: ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS
6.1 Profits and losses shall be allocated in proportion to Membership Interests.
6.2 Distributions shall be made as determined by the Manager.

ARTICLE VII: INDEMNIFICATION
7.1 The Company shall indemnify the Manager and Members to the fullest extent permitted by Florida law for actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company.

ARTICLE VIII: MISCELLANEOUS
8.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Florida.
8.2 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in [COUNTY], Florida.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Members have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.

__________________________
[Member Name]

   5.2 MSO shall maintain general business and professional liability insurance covering its non‑clinical services.

   6.1 This Agreement shall be governed by Florida law.

## SCREWWORM COMPLIANCE SERVICE SOP

```markdown
### Standard Operating Procedure: Screwworm Certificate for Mexico Returns

**Objective:** Ensure every dog returning from Mexico has a valid screwworm inspection certificate signed by a Mexican veterinary official within 5 days before re‑entry to the U.S., as required by USDA APHIS (9 CFR 93.600).

**Timeline:** The certificate must be issued no more than 5 days before the scheduled return flight.

**Step‑by‑Step Process:**

1. **Pre‑trip Coordination (7 days before departure):**
   - Confirm the return flight date and time with the client.
   - Contact the Mexican partner clinic to schedule the screwworm inspection appointment. The appointment must fall within the 5‑day window prior to return (ideally Day 2 or 3 of the trip).
   - Verify that the inspecting veterinarian is authorized by the Mexican government (SENASICA) to issue the certificate.

2. **During the Trip:**
   - On the day of the inspection, the concierge accompanies the client and pet to the clinic.
   - The veterinarian performs a physical examination focusing on skin, ears, and any wounds to detect screwworm larvae.
   - The certificate is completed on official letterhead (or the USDA‑approved bilingual form) and must include:
     - Pet owner’s name and address.
     - Pet’s identification (name, breed, age, microchip number).
     - Statement that the animal was inspected and found free of screwworm.
     - Date of inspection.
     - Signature and stamp of the veterinary official.
   - Obtain a digital scan/photo of the certificate immediately.

3. **Documentation Check (24 hours before return):**
   - Concierge verifies that the inspection date is within 5 days of the return flight.
   - The original certificate is placed with the pet’s travel documents.
   - A digital copy is uploaded to the client’s MedPup portal and emailed to the client.

4. **At U.S. Customs (CBP):**
   - The client (or concierge) presents the screwworm certificate along with the USDA‑endorsed health certificate.
   - The CBP officer may visually inspect the pet; certificate must be readily available.

5. **Post‑trip:**
   - Store the certificate in the client’s record for a minimum of 2 years.
   - If any issue arises with CBP, log the incident and update the SOP accordingly.

**Contingency:** If the inspection reveals a screwworm infestation, the pet cannot travel until treated and certified clear. MedPup will assist the client with extended stay arrangements and re‑booking.
```
