# Research Prompt for Third-Party Agent

---

## FULL CONTEXT

### What This Project Is

A Florida-based software engineer with no capital wants to build a business that routes cost-constrained pet owners to lower-cost veterinary care. The end-game model is a limited-scope surgical/dental center (like Dr. Kelly's Surgical Unit, which prices at 1/3 to 1/2 of full-service vets and operates 7 locations in AZ + Denver expansion). But Phase 1 is a referral/coordination business that can start with $0 — a landing page, manual routing to existing low-cost clinics, flat coordination fees.

### What We've Already Researched and Concluded

**Legal structure (validated):**
- Florida LLC for operations/referral business
- Licensed veterinarian must control clinical entity
- MSO structure separates management from medicine
- Flat admin/software/concierge fees are safe; per-procedure revenue share is not
- Fee-splitting is the biggest legal risk to avoid

**Model validation (Dr. Kelly's):**
- 7 locations in Arizona, Denver Fall 2026
- Dog spay/neuter: $160-$362 (vs FL $300-$800)
- Cat spay/neuter: $102-$125 (vs FL $200-$500)
- Dental cleaning: ~$900 (vs FL $1,500-$4,000)
- Prices = ~1/3 to 1/2 of full-service
- Break-even: 9-16 cases/day at ~$250-$450 avg revenue

**Consumer willingness (validated):**
- 71% of cost-constrained owners were never offered a cheaper alternative (Gallup 2025)
- Owners travel 2-6 miles normally — but will hand pets to concierge if savings are 50%+
- The concierge transport model (MexiVet Express, 80 pets/week, since 2018) proves demand exists

**Paths already researched and what was concluded:**
1. Texas land border (76.8% verified savings) — strongest margins but requires Texas presence
2. Puerto Rico (13.7% avg savings via Banfield data) — savings too small after air transport
3. Georgia drive (10% statewide, 37.6% Miami→rural GA) — only viable for spay referral to Thomasville nonprofit
4. Dr. Kelly's model (low-cost surgical center, validated) — best end-game but requires capital
5. Timeline/priority: Phase 1 referral business FIRST → prove demand → raise capital → Phase 2 clinic

### What We Still Need: Phase 1 Data

**The immediate question:** Where can a South Florida resident get a dental cleaning, spay/neuter, or mass removal for significantly less than full-service vet prices — RIGHT NOW — and would those clinics accept referred cases?

## RESEARCH PROMPT: South Florida Low-Cost Veterinary Landscape

### Goal

Find every low-cost veterinary clinic, mobile vet, spay/neuter clinic, dental-only service, or surgical center operating in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties that offers prices below full-service market rates. Also find pricing data for those clinics. Then identify which ones might accept referred cases from a coordination platform.

### Don't Re-Search These (Already Known)

- Dr. Kelly's Surgical Unit (Arizona/Denver — not in Florida)
- Texas border clinics (already verified)

### Methodology: Search For

**1. All Low-Cost Veterinary Options in South Florida**

Search for the following in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties:
- "low cost veterinary clinic Miami" "Miami affordable vet dental cleaning"
- "low cost spay neuter Fort Lauderdale" "Broward county low cost vet"
- "Palm Beach low cost veterinary clinic" "West Palm Beach affordable vet"
- "Miami low cost dog dental cleaning" "discount vet Miami"
- "South Florida veterinary discount clinic" "low income vet Miami"

Also search for specific known organizations that run low-cost clinics in South Florida:
- "ASPCA Miami community veterinary clinic" (we know they do $80 dog spay — is this still operating?)
- "Humane Society Broward low cost vet"
- "Miami-Dade Animal Services low cost clinic"
- "Peggy Adams Animal Rescue low cost clinic"
- "Spay Florida clinics list"
- "Pet MD Miami low cost"
- "Veterinary discount plans South Florida"

For each clinic found, collect:
- Name, address, phone, website
- Type: nonprofit, government-subsidized, private low-cost, mobile
- Services offered (dental, spay/neuter, surgery, or wellness only)
- Any published pricing (specific numbers preferred)
- Hours of operation
- Do they accept walk-ins or require appointments?
- Geographic coverage (which neighborhoods/cities)
- Do they have capacity for additional volume?

**2. Published Pricing for South Florida Low-Cost Clinics**

For each clinic found with published pricing, collect a table:

| Clinic | Dental cleaning | Spay (dog) | Neuter (dog) | Mass removal | Office visit |
|--------|----------------|------------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| [Name] | [price] | [price] | [price] | [price or N/A] | [price] |

Compare these to the Florida full-service averages (from prior research):
- Full-service FL office visit: ~$79.50 (MarketWatch)
- Full-service FL dental cleaning: ~$475 (CanineBible)
- Full-service FL spay: ~$400-$800
- Full-service FL mass removal: $800-$2,500

Calculate the savings percentage for each:
(Full_Service_Price - Low_Cost_Price) / Full_Service_Price × 100 = Savings%

**3. Mobile/Community Clinic Schedules in South Florida**

Search for:
- "mobile vet clinic Miami schedule 2026" "vet community clinic schedule Broward"
- "pop up vet clinic Miami Dade County 2026 2025"
- "vaccination clinic Miami" "low cost vaccine clinic for dogs Fort Lauderdale"
- "Spay Express" or "SpayMobile" or similar mobile surgical units in South Florida

These are important because they often have the lowest prices (subsidized) but limited schedules. A coordination business could route clients to upcoming pop-up clinics.

**4. Which Clinics Might Accept Referral Partnerships?**

This requires inference from public data since we can't call. Look for:
- Clinics that explicitly mention "high volume" or "appointments available" (signals capacity)
- Clinics that have "low cost" in their name or mission (signals alignment)
- Clinics that are NOT full-service (signals they focus on specific procedures)
- Private practices in lower-income areas (may be more price-sensitive)
- Any clinic that mentions "referrals welcome" or "partnering with local organizations"

For each, rate as HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW likelihood of accepting referral partnerships based on public signals.

**5. Dr. Kelly's Full Price Menu (for comparison)**

We have spay/neuter prices. Try to find the complete Dr. Kelly's price list including:
- Dental cleaning (with and without extractions)
- Mass removal
- Orthopedic procedures
- Any other surgical procedures offered

Their website is drkellysvet.com and they have a /prices/ page and /faq/ page.

Also search:
- "Dr. Kelly's Surgical Unit dental cleaning price"
- "Dr. Kelly's Surgical Unit price list 2025 2026"
- "Dr. Kelly's Surgical Unit mass removal cost"

**6. Gap Analysis: What Procedures Are Underserved in South Florida?**

Looking at the landscape of low-cost clinics found in #1:
- Dental cleaning: which clinics offer it at reduced rates? (Many spay/neuter clinics DON'T do dental)
- Spay/neuter: which clinics offer it? (Most common low-cost service)
- Mass removal: which clinics do this? (Rare in low-cost settings)
- Orthopedic: ANY clinic offering reduced-cost orthopedics? (Almost certainly not)

Identify the specific gaps where a referral/coordination business could add the most value.

## THE KEY QUESTION THIS RESEARCH ANSWERS

**"Where can a South Florida pet owner get lower-cost dental, spay, or surgery TODAY — and is the demand large enough to support a coordination business that routes people to those clinics?"**
