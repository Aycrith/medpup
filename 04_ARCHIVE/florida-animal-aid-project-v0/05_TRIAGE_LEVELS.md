# Triage Levels

## Important Boundary

These levels are for non-diagnostic urgency sorting based on reported observations. They are not veterinary diagnosis, prognosis, or treatment advice.

## Level 0 — Immediate Emergency

### Examples

- severe trouble breathing,
- collapse or unconsciousness,
- active seizure or repeated seizures,
- severe bleeding,
- hit by car or major trauma,
- suspected poisoning,
- inability to urinate,
- bloated abdomen with distress,
- extreme pain,
- pale, white, blue, or gray gums,
- heatstroke,
- birthing emergency,
- severe wounds,
- major eye trauma.

### Action

- Tell requester to contact or go to an emergency veterinarian immediately.
- Offer transport coordination only if it does not delay care.
- Do not attempt remote medical management.
- Mark case `Emergency advised`.
- Log advice and follow up.

### Standard Message

> This may be an emergency. We are not a veterinary clinic and cannot diagnose or treat. Please contact or go to an emergency veterinarian now.

---

## Level 1 — Urgent Same-Day Veterinary Review Needed

### Examples

- lethargy with not eating,
- worsening infection signs,
- moderate wounds,
- repeated vomiting/diarrhea,
- limping with pain,
- suspected urinary issue without confirmed blockage,
- respiratory symptoms not yet severe,
- sick puppies or kittens,
- senior animal sudden decline,
- painful swelling,
- eye problems without major trauma.

### Action

- Route to same-day vet, urgent clinic, rescue medical partner, or licensed veterinary review.
- Collect photos/video if available.
- Help with transport coordination or cost-resource search.
- Schedule follow-up within 24 hours.

### Standard Message

> Based on what you reported, this should be reviewed by a licensed veterinarian as soon as possible, ideally today. We cannot diagnose or treat, but we can help organize the details and look for appropriate care options.

---

## Level 2 — Soon / Non-Emergent Vet or Rescue Support

### Examples

- minor skin concerns,
- routine wellness needs,
- vaccine access,
- spay/neuter access,
- parasite concerns,
- rehoming risk,
- owner hardship,
- food/supplies need,
- mild behavior concern without immediate danger,
- stable chronic issue needing appointment help.

### Action

- Match to clinic, low-cost resource, rescue, shelter-diversion resource, food pantry, or owner-support resource.
- Generate case summary if useful.
- Schedule follow-up within 2–7 days.

### Standard Message

> This does not appear from the information provided to require emergency routing, but a licensed veterinarian or appropriate animal resource may still be needed. We can help identify options and organize next steps.

---

## Level 3 — Social / Logistics Case

### Examples

- owner lacks transportation,
- temporary pet food need,
- housing instability,
- pet retention help,
- lost/found pet guidance,
- foster search,
- owner overwhelmed by resource navigation,
- need for appointment scheduling help.

### Action

- Use non-medical support workflow.
- Match to social, transport, rescue, shelter, food, or community support resources.
- Track outcome.
- Escalate if new medical red flags appear.

### Standard Message

> We can help with non-medical coordination, resource matching, and follow-up. If any emergency medical signs appear, contact an emergency veterinarian immediately.

---

## Unknown / Insufficient Information

If the available information is unclear:

- ask only the minimum questions needed to identify emergency red flags,
- do not imply safety,
- provide emergency warning,
- request photos/video only if it does not delay urgent care,
- err toward licensed veterinary review.

### Message

> We do not have enough information to determine the best resource. We cannot diagnose or tell you it is safe to wait. If any emergency signs are present, contact an emergency veterinarian now. Otherwise, please answer these questions so we can help route the case.

## Triage Audit Fields

Every case should record:

- triage level,
- red flags yes/no,
- red flag details,
- who assigned level,
- time assigned,
- message sent,
- next action,
- follow-up due date.
