# Transport Protocol

## Purpose

This protocol governs non-medical animal transport coordination.

The project is not a veterinary ambulance, emergency medical service, shelter transport department, or animal control agency.

## Transport Eligibility

Transport may be considered only when:

- the destination has been confirmed,
- the owner/custodian consents,
- the animal can be safely handled,
- the animal can be safely contained,
- transport does not delay emergency veterinary care,
- the driver is available and willing,
- weather and vehicle conditions are safe,
- the case is within the pilot area or an approved exception.

## Transport Decline Criteria

Do not transport if:

- the animal is aggressive or cannot be safely contained,
- the animal is medically unstable and professional emergency transport is needed,
- the requester has not confirmed consent,
- the destination has not accepted the animal,
- the animal may be subject to legal custody issues,
- the case involves wildlife requiring specialized handling,
- the driver is not equipped,
- the trip would create unsafe delay,
- the operator is uncomfortable with the risk.

## Required Pre-Transport Checklist

Before pickup:

- [ ] Case ID exists.
- [ ] Destination confirmed.
- [ ] Owner/custodian consent confirmed.
- [ ] Animal identity/description confirmed.
- [ ] Pickup address confirmed.
- [ ] Dropoff address confirmed.
- [ ] Contact at destination confirmed.
- [ ] Animal handling risk assessed.
- [ ] Crate/carrier/restraint available.
- [ ] Vehicle ventilation adequate.
- [ ] Cleaning supplies available.
- [ ] Emergency contact number available.
- [ ] Pickup/dropoff times planned.

## Containment Rules

Preferred:

- hard-sided crate,
- secure carrier,
- appropriate leash/slip lead for dogs,
- pet seatbelt/restraint where appropriate.

Do not transport loose animals in the vehicle.

Cats should be in carriers unless there is no safe alternative and a partner has advised otherwise.

Unknown animals should not be handled without proper containment.

## During Transport

Driver should:

- keep animal contained,
- avoid unnecessary stops,
- avoid opening carrier/crate in unsecured areas,
- keep temperature safe,
- document major behavior or health changes,
- call destination if arrival changes,
- avoid medical intervention beyond basic safety actions.

## After Transport

Operator/driver must:

- confirm dropoff,
- log dropoff time,
- record person/organization receiving animal,
- upload receipt/confirmation if available,
- clean/disinfect vehicle/crate,
- record incidents,
- update case status,
- schedule follow-up.

## Incident Report Required If

- bite/scratch occurs,
- animal escapes,
- vehicle accident occurs,
- animal worsens during transport,
- destination refuses intake,
- owner disputes consent,
- property damage occurs,
- driver feels unsafe.

## Transport Consent Draft Language

> I authorize Florida Animal Aid Access Project or its designated volunteer/helper to assist with non-medical transportation coordination for this animal. I understand the project is not a veterinary clinic, animal ambulance, animal control agency, or emergency medical service. I understand the project does not diagnose, treat, prescribe, or guarantee outcomes. I confirm that I have authority to request transport for this animal and consent to transport to the listed destination.

## Acceptance Criteria

- No transport occurs without destination confirmation.
- No transport occurs without owner/custodian consent.
- No loose animals are transported.
- Aggressive/unstable animals are declined or referred.
- Pickup and dropoff times are logged.
- Vehicle/crate cleaning is logged.
