# Mission Statement

## Working Name

Florida Animal Aid Access Project

## Public Mission

Florida Animal Aid Access Project helps Florida pets and people reach appropriate help faster through non-veterinary intake, documentation, referral coordination, transportation coordination, and follow-up.

## Plain-English Version

We help people figure out where to go, what information to gather, how to contact the right animal support resource, and how to move a case forward without pretending to be a veterinary clinic.

## Internal Mission

Build a legally conservative, operationally lean animal aid coordination layer that reduces avoidable suffering by helping owners, finders, rescuers, shelters, and veterinary providers exchange better information and move cases faster.

## Core Commitments

1. Help animals quickly without overbuilding.
2. Stay inside non-veterinary legal boundaries.
3. Route medical issues to licensed veterinary providers.
4. Reduce chaos for owners, vets, shelters, and rescues.
5. Document cases clearly.
6. Follow up instead of abandoning unresolved cases.
7. Add new capabilities only after policies, insurance, funding, and partner support exist.

## What Success Looks Like in the Pilot

The pilot succeeds if it can:

- receive animal-help requests,
- identify emergency red flags,
- route emergencies to emergency veterinary care,
- organize non-emergency cases into clean summaries,
- match cases to appropriate local resources,
- coordinate safe non-medical transport where available,
- track outcomes,
- and avoid unauthorized veterinary practice.

## What Success Does Not Require at Launch

The pilot does not need:

- a clinic,
- veterinary staff,
- a shelter facility,
- public fundraising,
- paid staff,
- a foster network,
- statewide coverage,
- custom software,
- or a large volunteer base.

## One-Sentence Operating Doctrine

Start as the coordination system that makes every existing animal welfare resource easier to access.
