# Service Boundary Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines what Florida Animal Aid Access Project may and may not do during the launch pilot.

The project exists to provide non-veterinary coordination, documentation, referral, transport support, and case follow-up. It does not provide veterinary medicine.

## Required Public Boundary Language

Use this language on the website, intake form, case summaries, partner outreach, social pages, and automated messages:

> We are not a veterinary clinic. We cannot diagnose, prescribe, or treat. We help document the issue and connect you with licensed veterinarians, shelters, rescues, and other animal support resources.

For urgent cases:

> If your animal is having trouble breathing, collapsing, seizing, bleeding severely, unable to urinate, possibly poisoned, hit by a car, in heatstroke, or in extreme pain, contact or go to an emergency veterinarian immediately.

## Allowed Launch Services

The project may provide:

- structured intake forms,
- observable-fact urgency sorting,
- general non-diagnostic owner education,
- referral to licensed veterinary providers,
- referral to shelters, rescues, animal control, fosters, or support organizations,
- transportation coordination to confirmed destinations,
- help finding low-cost veterinary resources,
- appointment scheduling assistance,
- emergency escalation guidance,
- pet food/supply support,
- temporary foster coordination only after written foster policy exists,
- lost/found pet workflow coordination,
- humane trap coordination through proper partners,
- microchip scan coordination through lawful/equipped providers,
- basic husbandry and containment guidance,
- sanitation guidance,
- photo/video documentation for licensed vet/rescue review,
- case tracking,
- follow-up reminders.

## Prohibited Without Licensed Veterinary Authority

The project must not provide:

- diagnosis,
- prognosis,
- prescription advice,
- medication dosing instructions,
- medication administration,
- vaccination services,
- wound treatment beyond basic safety guidance,
- euthanasia,
- dentistry,
- surgery,
- anesthesia,
- medical procedures,
- veterinary telehealth,
- medical triage represented as veterinary triage,
- claims that imply licensed veterinary services.

## Marketing Words to Avoid

Avoid describing the project as:

- clinic,
- veterinary service,
- mobile vet,
- animal urgent care,
- medical triage provider,
- treatment provider,
- veterinary telehealth,
- veterinary technician service,
- emergency service,
- hospital,
- rescue intake facility,
- shelter.

## Safer Positioning Words

Use:

- animal aid coordination,
- owner support,
- referral coordination,
- transport coordination,
- field documentation support,
- resource navigation,
- case-management support,
- rescue/shelter/vet routing,
- non-medical support.

## Staff/Volunteer Script

When someone asks for medical advice:

> I’m sorry, but we cannot diagnose, prescribe, or advise treatment. We can help organize the facts, document what you are seeing, and connect you with a licensed veterinarian or emergency clinic.

When someone asks whether they can wait:

> We cannot determine whether it is safe to wait. If you are seeing emergency signs such as breathing trouble, collapse, seizures, severe bleeding, inability to urinate, suspected poisoning, major trauma, heatstroke, or extreme pain, please contact or go to an emergency veterinarian now.

When someone asks for medication dosing:

> We cannot give medication dosing instructions. Please contact a licensed veterinarian, emergency clinic, or poison-control resource where appropriate.

## Boundary Violation Log

Any possible boundary issue should be logged with:

- date/time,
- case ID,
- what was requested,
- what was said,
- whether emergency care was advised,
- whether a licensed provider was contacted,
- follow-up action.

## Review Trigger

Review this policy before adding any of the following:

- donation-funded medical aid,
- volunteer drivers,
- foster placements,
- animal custody,
- vaccination/microchip events,
- mobile veterinary partnerships,
- sponsored-care payments,
- public fundraising,
- paid services,
- or county-wide expansion.
