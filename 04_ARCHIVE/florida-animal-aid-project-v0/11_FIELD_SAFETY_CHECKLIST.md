# Field Safety Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist governs basic non-medical field documentation and transport-support activity.

The project should avoid unnecessary field response during the first pilot. Field work creates bite, injury, disease, traffic, liability, and scope-creep risk.

## Before Any Field Activity

- [ ] Case ID exists.
- [ ] Location confirmed.
- [ ] Purpose of field activity is non-medical.
- [ ] Emergency red flags assessed.
- [ ] If emergency, veterinary care is prioritized.
- [ ] Animal ownership/custody situation clarified.
- [ ] Bite/aggression risk assessed.
- [ ] Another person knows your location.
- [ ] Phone charged.
- [ ] PPE available.
- [ ] Crate/carrier/restraint available if transport is possible.
- [ ] Destination confirmed if transport is planned.
- [ ] Consent confirmed if handling or transport is planned.

## Do Not Handle

Do not handle:

- aggressive animals,
- animals that cannot be safely contained,
- wildlife requiring specialized handling,
- animals involved in active legal/custody disputes,
- animals in dangerous traffic locations without proper authority,
- animals that may expose you to serious bite/scratch risk,
- medically unstable animals that require professional emergency care.

## Basic Field Kit

- slip leads,
- leashes/collars,
- gloves,
- towels/blankets,
- disposable pee pads,
- crates/carriers,
- pet seatbelt/restraint,
- cleaning supplies,
- animal-safe disinfectant,
- trash bags,
- flashlight/headlamp,
- clipboard/forms,
- portable phone charger,
- PPE,
- bite-resistant gloves if handling unknown animals,
- microchip scanner if budget allows,
- water bowl,
- spare phone cable.

## Sanitation

After each field/transport case:

- remove disposable waste,
- wash hands,
- disinfect hard surfaces,
- clean crates/carriers,
- launder towels/blankets,
- avoid cross-contamination between animals,
- log cleaning if transport occurred.

## Personal Safety Rules

- Do not enter private property without permission.
- Do not enter unsafe buildings/yards.
- Do not confront hostile people.
- Do not handle animals alone when risk is unclear.
- Do not block traffic.
- Do not impersonate animal control.
- Do not imply law enforcement authority.
- Leave if the scene becomes unsafe.

## Bite/Scratch Incident

If bite/scratch occurs:

1. Move to safety.
2. Wash wound.
3. Seek medical care if needed.
4. Document incident.
5. Report according to local requirements if applicable.
6. Update case risk status.
7. Suspend similar handling until protocol review.

## Acceptance Criteria

- No field activity occurs without a case record.
- No transport occurs without consent and destination confirmation.
- No aggressive/unstable animal is handled casually.
- Incidents are documented.
- Cleaning occurs after every transport.
