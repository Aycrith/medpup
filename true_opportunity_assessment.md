# MedPup / AnimalAid — True Opportunity Assessment
## Fresh Analysis: What Models Actually Work in the Pet Cost Crisis
**Date:** May 19, 2026

**Premise:** The US veterinary cost crisis is real (47% since 2020, BLS confirmed; 50%+ owners forgoing care). The question isn't whether demand exists — it's whether a repeatable, profitable business model can serve it. This document assesses every viable model against real-world data.

## Part 1: The Proven Model — Land-Border Mexico (What Works Today)

### MexiVet Express — The Only Scaled Success

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Volume** | 80 pets/week (4,160/year) |
| **Location** | San Diego ↔ Tijuana (Otay Mesa) |
| **Model** | Concierge — handles scheduling, logistics, paperwork, transport, coord w/ partner vets |
| **Years operating** | Since ~2019 (5-7 years, still operating) |
| **Procedures** | Dental, spay/neuter, orthopedics, oncology, diagnostics |
| **Pricing** | Not public, but 70-80% savings vs US for procedures |
| **Revenue model** | Likely flat fee per pet + markup on procedure costs |
| **Competition** | None at scale — only individual clinics |

**Key insight:** MexiVet Express is the ONLY scaled pet medical tourism platform in North America. There is NO equivalent for the Texas border, Arizona border, or Florida-to-Puerto Rico route. This is a gap, not a validation that the market doesn't support it.

### Price Savings — Documented (Not Estimated)

| Procedure | US Price | Mexico Land Border | Savings | 
|-----------|---------|--------------------|---------|
| Dental cleaning | $800-$1,500 | $200-$400 | **70-75%** |
| Spay | $500-$900 | $100-$250 | **70-80%** |
| ACL repair (CCL) | $3,500-$6,000 | $800-$1,500 | **75-80%** |

At 70-80% savings, the concierge has room to take a 20-30% margin and still leave the client saving 40-50% vs US pricing. This math WORKS — unlike the $900 concierge fee on $600 base costs we were calculating for Freeport and Cancún air.

### Why the Land Border Works (vs Air to Cancún)

| Factor | Air to Cancún | Land Border (Texas) |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Transport cost per pet | $220-$500 RT | **$0-40** (drive across) |

## Part 2: Other Viable Models (Ranked)

### Tier 1: Puerto Rico Pet Medical Tourism (Florida-based)

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Customs** | **None** — US territory. $0 customs/APHIS |
| **Flights from FL** | MIA/FLL to SJU: 2.5-3h, $80-$200 RT. JetBlue, American, Spirit, Frontier |
| **Airlines pet fees** | $125-$200 each way |
| **Vet costs** | 40-60% lower than Florida (anecdotal reports: dental $150-$300 vs FL $400-$1,200) |
| **Vet quality** | San Juan metro has modern animal hospitals, bilingual vets |
| **Screwworm** | No issue (US territory, same status) |
| **Existing packages** | **NONE** — zero companies offer this |
| **Verdict** | **HIGH OPPORTUNITY** — easiest import, real cost gap, untapped |

**Why this could work:** A Florida pet owner spends $300 RT on pet flights + $150 hotel + $250 dental procedure = $700 total, vs $1,000-$1,500 for the same procedure in Florida. Even at a $200 concierge fee, the client saves $100-$600. The margins are real.

**Why it doesn't exist yet:** Lack of organized platform. Individual vets in PR don't market to US clients. The paperwork barrier (airline health certificate) is low but needs a facilitator.

### Tier 2: Domestic US Pet Medical Tourism (Cheaper States)

| Destination | Procedure cost vs FL | Driveable from FL? |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Georgia** | 30-50% lower | ✅ Yes (ATL 6h from FLL) |
| **Alabama** | 35-55% lower | ✅ Yes (BHM 10h from FLL) |
| **Texas (border cities)** | 40-60% lower | ❌ Far (El Paso 28h from FLL) |
| **Puerto Rico** | 40-60% lower | ❌ Fly only |

**Opportunity:** Partner with a high-volume, low-cost clinic in **Georgia** (6-hour drive from South Florida). Bundle: vet appointment + same-day return. No airfare, no customs, no paperwork beyond a standard appointment. Target: dental cleanings, spay/neuter, basic mass removals.

**Why this could work:** $0 transport (drive), $0 customs, $0 paperwork. Pure arbitrage on procedure cost — 30-50% lower in Georgia means a $400 dental cleaning in FL costs $200 in Georgia. Even at a modest markup, the client saves money.

---

### Tier 3: Pet Dental Subscription Membership

**Model:** Monthly subscription ($15-$30/month) gives members access to discounted dental cleanings at partner clinics, annual X-rays, and 10-20% off procedures.

**Why this works:** Human dental discount plans are a $10B+ industry. Pets have the same problem: dental is the most commonly deferred procedure due to cost. A pet dental subscription could partner with existing low-cost clinics and bundle cleanings into a predictable monthly payment.

**Existing precedent:** Petedent (startup), VCA CareClub, Banfield Optimum Wellness — all prove the subscription model works in pet care.

---

### Tier 4: B2B — Pet Insurance Partnership

**Model:** Partner with a pet insurance carrier to create a "cost-effective care network" that incentivizes members to use lower-cost clinics in exchange for higher reimbursement rates.

**Why this could work:** Pet insurance penetration is only 5-6% vs 25-35% in the UK/Sweden. The market is growing at 17% CAGR to $10B by 2030. Insurers are looking for ways to control costs. A network of pre-vetted, low-cost providers (in Puerto Rico, Georgia, or border Mexico) could be a legitimate cost-control tool for insurers.

## Part 3: The Cancún + Freeport Reality Check

With the full data now assembled, here's where the original MedPup model sits:

| Hub | Best-case margin per pet | Required fee | Verdict |
|-----|--------------------------|-------------|---------|
| **Freeport** | $150-$300 (ferry $300+permit+HC+vet) | $900 | **Thin margin, no orthopedic confirmed** |
| **Cancún air** | $125-$320 (air $220-$500+prep+HC+vet) | $900-$1,200 | **Thin margin, air cost kills savings** |
| **Texas land border** | $400-$1,200 (drive+easy import+70% savings) | $500-$1,500 | **Proven model, 70-80% savings** |
| **Puerto Rico air** | $200-$600 (flight+hotel+40-60% savings) | $800-$1,200 | **Best option for Florida, untapped** |
| **Georgia drive** | $100-$300 (drive only, 30-50% savings) | $600-$900 | **Most accessible, lowest complexity** |

**The data says:** Air-based pet medical tourism (Freeport, Cancún) has margins too thin to work at $900 concierge fee. The land-border model works because transport costs are near-zero. Puerto Rico works because the cost gap is real and the import barrier is zero.

## Part 5: The Real Opportunity

### If you want the simplest, lowest-risk path:

**Georgia pet procedure partnership.** Drive 6 hours from South Florida, partner with a single high-volume low-cost clinic, bundle appointments for dental/wellness/spay. Zero customs, zero APHIS, zero screwworm. Transport = gas money ($50-$80). Downside: savings are 30-50%, not 70-80%. But the operational complexity is essentially zero.

### If you want the highest-margin, proven model:

**Texas land-border platform.** El Paso-Juárez, Brownsville-Matamoros, or Laredo-Nuevo Laredo. Partner with established border clinics already serving US clients. 70-80% savings support a 20-30% take rate. But you'd need to be in Texas or hire a Texas-based coordinator. The model is proven by MexiVet Express at 80 pets/week.

### If you want a Florida-appropriate, scalable model:

**Florida ↔ Puerto Rico pet medical tourism.** Direct flights, no customs, no screwworm, no APHIS. REAL 40-60% cost savings on procedures. Zero companies doing this — completely untapped. The import barrier is low (just an airline health certificate). The main operational lift is vet clinic vetting and client logistics coordination.

### If you want something entirely different:

**Pet dental subscription + travel.** Own a membership model ($20/month) that gives members access to discounted dental cleanings at partner clinics in Puerto Rico or Georgia. Monthly recurring revenue + per-service fees from partner clinics. Scale to 1,000+ members in year one and you have a repeatable business.
