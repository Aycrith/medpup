### 1. CRITICAL LEGAL & REGULATORY KNOWLEDGE BASES

These are the specific legal statutes, regulatory frameworks, and compliance documents the agent must understand, monitor, and operationalize.

**1.1 USDA APHIS Pet Import Regulatory Framework**

The agent must ingest and maintain a living knowledge base of the exact regulatory requirements for pet re-entry to the United States:

- **Screwworm Inspection Mandate:** The Code of Federal Regulations (9 CFR 93.600) requires that all dogs entering the U.S. from screwworm-affected regions must be accompanied by a certificate signed by a full-time salaried veterinary official of the region of origin, stating the dog was inspected for screwworm within **5 days preceding shipment** to the United States. The agent must operationalize this as a hard deadline with automated countdown reminders.

- **Healthy Dog Importation Act (HR 3349):** Pending legislation that expands USDA electronic health documentation requirements for all dogs entering the United States. The agent must monitor this bill's progress and pre-build the electronic documentation infrastructure to ensure compliance before it becomes law.

- **APHIS eFile System:** Import permits for live animals must be applied for via the APHIS eFile system, with APHIS requiring at least **7 business days** to process routine applications. The agent must incorporate this lead time into trip planning workflows.

- **Country-Specific Requirements by Destination:**
  - **Mexico:** Screwworm-affected since November 22, 2024. Certificate required within 5 days of return. USDA-endorsed health certificate required.
  - **Bahamas:** Not screwworm-affected. No USDA endorsement required for health certificates. Import permit obtained via Click2Clear ePermit system at a fee of $10.00 plus 12% VAT ($11.20).
  - **Jamaica:** Not on CDC high-risk list for dog rabies. Pets must enter only through Kingston or Palisadoes Airport. Import permit required.
  - **Cuba:** On CDC high-risk list for dog rabies. Requires rabies vaccination certificate, microchip, CDC Dog Import Form, and potentially rabies serology titer.
  - **Panama:** Mandatory 40-day home quarantine. $130 fee. Screwworm-affected.

**1.2 Florida Corporate Practice of Veterinary Medicine & MSO-PC Structure**

The agent must understand and implement the precise legal architecture required:

- **The Core Structure:** The PC must be 100% owned by a licensed veterinarian. The MSO provides non-clinical management, administrative, and support services. A Management Services Agreement (MSA) governs the relationship, with the MSO receiving a management fee that must be set at Fair Market Value to avoid regulatory scrutiny.

- **The Red Flags:** The medical practice (Friendly PC) must retain ultimate control over all clinical decisions. Any clause in the MSA that gives the MSO control over clinical operations renders the structure legally non-compliant.

- **"Practice of Veterinary Medicine" Definition (Florida Statute 474.201):** The agent must understand that Florida law defines the practice of veterinary medicine as diagnosing the medical condition of animals and prescribing, dispensing, or administering drugs. The MedPup MSO must never cross this line.

**1.3 CareCredit Provider Agreement Knowledge Base**

- The CareCredit credit card offers promotional 0% APR periods on qualifying purchases if paid in full within 6, 12, 18, or 24 months.
- CareCredit can be used at 285,000+ enrolled provider and select retail locations. The agent must verify that Freeport Veterinary Hospital's CareCredit acceptance remains active before each trip.
- The agent must monitor for any changes to the CareCredit provider agreement, particularly the third-party billing prohibition, and flag any updates for human review.
