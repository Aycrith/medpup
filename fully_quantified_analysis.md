## Model A: Puerto Rico Pet Medical Tourism

### The Opportunity

**Why it matters:** Puerto Rico is a US territory. This means:
- ✅ **No customs, no APHIS, no screwworm, no import permit** — pets from mainland are domestic travel
- ✅ **Direct flights** from MIA/FLL to SJU: 2.5 hours, 6+ airlines
- ✅ **US currency, US legal system** — no foreign exchange or liability concerns
- ✅ **40-60% lower vet costs** than mainland US
- ✅ **Bilingual vets** — most San Juan vets speak English
- ✅ **$0 competitor presence** — no company currently offers this

## Model D & E: Freeport Ferry + Cancún Air (Original Models)

With the full data now available:

| Metric | Freeport | Cancún |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Transport cost/pet | $300 (ferry) ✅ | $220–$500 (air) ✅ |
| Import permit | $50 ✅ | $50–$100 ⚠️ |
| APHIS/screwworm | $0 (no APHIS) ✅ | $50–$100 (APHIS + screwworm) ✅ |

| Total cost/pet | $600–$1,050 | $645–$1,430 |
| Best margin at $900 fee | $300/pet (best case) | $320/pet (best case) |
| **Cushion for cost overruns** | **❌ None** | **❌ None** |
| Proven surgical capability | ❌ No ortho confirmed | ✅ Ortho confirmed |
| Vet quality confidence | Low | High |

**Verdict:** Neither model clears the margin threshold needed for a sustainable business. The transport cost alone (ferry $300, air $220-$500) eats up the entire pricing advantage before the vet procedure even happens. The land border and Puerto Rico models win because transport cost is near-zero or much lower relative to savings.

## Recommendation Path

**The quantitative data clearly favors:**
1. **Puerto Rico** — best option for a Florida-based operator. Untapped, US territory, real savings, no customs. Partner with 1-2 San Juan clinics.
2. **Texas land border** — highest margin but requires TX presence or investment. Proven by MexiVet Express.
3. **Georgia drive** — lowest friction, simplest to test. Single van trip to Valdosta, GA with 4 pets proves the model.
