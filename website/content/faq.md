## Safety & Quality

**Is the quality of veterinary care the same as the U.S.?**
Yes. Our partner clinics employ board-certified surgeons, use WHO Surgical Safety Checklists, and maintain advanced imaging and surgical equipment comparable to top U.S. specialty hospitals. Every clinic is inspected in person by MedPup before partnership.

**How do you vet your partner clinics?**
We conduct a thorough on-site audit of every partner clinic using our inspection checklist. We verify surgeon credentials against government databases, inspect surgical suites, confirm equipment standards, and review safety protocols.

**What if something goes wrong during surgery?**
Your pet is under the care of a licensed veterinarian at all times. The clinic has full emergency protocols in place. Our concierge is on-site throughout the procedure and is the communication bridge between the surgical team and you. If you're not present, we contact you immediately.

## Travel & Logistics

**Do I have to travel with my dog?**
For Freeport day-trips, you're welcome to join — the 3-hour ferry is pet-friendly and comfortable. For Cancun surgical trips, you can either join the group charter or your dog travels with our trained concierge while you stay in Florida.

**What paperwork is needed?**
We handle 100% of the paperwork. Typically needed: a USDA-accredited health certificate (obtained from your regular vet within 10 days of travel), current rabies vaccination certificate, and destination-specific import permits. We guide you through every step.

**How long is the trip?**
Freeport is a single day: depart Port Everglades at 7 AM, return by 8 PM. Cancun is 2-4 nights depending on the procedure and recovery needs.

## Financing & Insurance

**Can I use CareCredit?**
Yes — CareCredit is accepted directly at our Freeport partner clinic. You can use your existing CareCredit card for the surgical fees. Our concierge fee is paid separately via card or ACH.

**Does pet insurance cover international surgery?**
Some pet insurance providers cover procedures performed internationally. We recommend contacting your provider before booking. We provide complete surgical reports and invoices for your claim submission.

**What if I need to cancel?**
Our concierge fee is refundable (less a $50 processing fee) if cancelled more than 7 days before the trip. Within 7 days, it's non-refundable. Surgical fees are paid directly to the clinic and subject to their cancellation policy.

## Veterinary Partners

**Will my regular vet still be involved?**
Absolutely. Your primary U.S. veterinarian is a critical part of the process. They provide the initial diagnosis and health certificate, and they handle all post-operative follow-up. We send them a complete surgical report and remain available for any questions.

**Can I speak with the surgeon beforehand?**
Yes. We facilitate a pre-surgical consult between you (and your U.S. vet if desired) and the operating surgeon. You can ask any questions about the procedure, recovery, and aftercare.

## Medical Suitability

**Is my dog a candidate?**
Most dogs in good general health are candidates. We start with a free consultation to review your dog's diagnosis, medical history, and the recommended procedure. We'll let you know if a MedPup trip is appropriate.

**Are there any dogs that shouldn't travel?**
Dogs with unstable medical conditions, advanced heart disease, or certain respiratory conditions may not be suitable for travel. Your U.S. veterinarian is best positioned to advise on this. We always recommend a pre-travel wellness exam.
