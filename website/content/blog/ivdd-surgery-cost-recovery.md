If your dog has been diagnosed with IVDD (Intervertebral Disc Disease), you're facing one of the most expensive veterinary surgeries in the U.S. — and one of the most time-sensitive. **IVDD surgery costs $10,000 to $15,000** in the United States, and the window for successful treatment can be as narrow as 24 to 48 hours.

---

## What Is IVDD?

IVDD occurs when the cushioning discs between your dog's vertebrae rupture or bulge, pressing on the spinal cord. It's most common in long-backed breeds like Dachshunds, Corgis, and Basset Hounds, but can affect any dog.

**Hansen Type I IVDD (acute):** Sudden disc rupture. Most common in chondrodystrophic breeds (Dachshunds, French Bulldogs, Beagles). Requires emergency intervention.

**Hansen Type II IVDD (gradual):** Progressive disc bulging. More common in older, larger breeds. May respond to medical management.

---

## Signs Your Dog Needs Immediate Veterinary Attention

IVDD is graded on a 1-5 scale. Grades 3 and above require surgery:

| Grade | Signs | Urgency |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Neck/back pain only, normal walking | Can wait for vet appointment |
| 2 | Pain + weakness, wobbling when walking | See vet within 24 hours |
| 3 | Unable to walk but still feels pain in toes | **Emergency — surgery within 24-48 hours** |
| 4 | Unable to walk, no pain sensation | **Emergency — surgery within 24 hours** |
| 5 | Paralysis + no bladder control | **Critical emergency — hours matter** |

**If your dog suddenly can't walk, is dragging their back legs, or has lost bladder control: go to an emergency vet immediately.**

## Surgical Options

### Hemilaminectomy (Standard IVDD Surgery)
The most common procedure. The surgeon removes a portion of the vertebra to access and remove the ruptured disc material.

**U.S. cost:** $10,000 - $15,000 (specialty hospital, neurology team)

**What's included:**
- MRI or CT scan (required before surgery — $2,000-$3,500)
- Surgical decompression
- 3-7 day hospital stay
- Medications
- Follow-up imaging

### Ventral Slot (Cervical IVDD)
For discs in the neck region. The surgeon approaches through the underside of the neck.

**U.S. cost:** $8,000 - $12,000

## Recovery and Prognosis

| Factor | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Surgery within 24 hours of paralysis | 85-95% return to walking |
| Surgery within 48 hours | 75-85% return to walking |
| Surgery after 48 hours | 50-60% return to walking |
| Dogs with intact pain sensation (Grade 3) | Excellent prognosis |
| Dogs with no pain sensation (Grade 4-5) | Guarded — but many recover with aggressive treatment |

**Recovery timeline:**
- **Days 1-3:** Strict cage rest, pain management
- **Week 1-2:** Gradual introduction of supported standing and walking
- **Week 3-6:** Physical therapy (underwater treadmill, passive range of motion)
- **Week 6-12:** Progressive return to function
- **Month 3-6:** Most dogs reach their maximum recovery level

## Why Speed Matters

Every hour counts with IVDD. If your dog is Grade 3 or above:

1. **Call your regular vet** for an immediate referral to a neurologist
2. **Get an MRI** to confirm the diagnosis and locate the disc
3. **Schedule surgery** — the window is 24-48 hours from loss of function

The challenge is that most U.S. specialty hospitals require a $5,000-$10,000 deposit before surgery. This is where cost becomes a barrier to life-saving care.

## Prevention

While IVDD can't always be prevented, these steps reduce risk:

- **Weight management** — every extra pound stresses the spine
- **Ramps instead of stairs** — for getting on/off furniture and in/out of cars
- **Harness instead of collar** — reduces neck strain
- **No jumping** — discourage jumping on/off furniture
- **Core strengthening** — controlled walking on inclines, balance exercises

## The Bottom Line

IVDD is a medical emergency that requires fast action and significant financial resources. If your dog is diagnosed at Grade 1-2, you have time to plan and explore cost-saving options. At Grade 3+, the priority is getting surgery as quickly as possible.
