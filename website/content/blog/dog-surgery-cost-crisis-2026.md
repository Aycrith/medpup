If your vet quoted you $4,000-$6,000 for TPLO surgery or $1,500-$2,500 for dental cleaning, you're not alone. Veterinary costs in the United States have risen 47% since 2020 — nearly double the general inflation rate over the same period, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics veterinary CPI index.

**The numbers are stark:**
- 53% of pet owners say a $500-$2,500 vet bill is impossible to afford (Spot Pet Insurance, 2026)
- Over 50% of owners have delayed necessary care due to cost
- Only 4.6% of pet-owning households have pet insurance
- 36,670+ dogs crossed from the US into Mexico for veterinary care in 2025 alone (a 11.6% YoY increase)

### Why is vet care so expensive?

Three structural factors are driving costs:

1. **Corporate consolidation** — Private equity firms have acquired thousands of independent vet practices, standardizing higher pricing across networks
2. **Advanced diagnostics** — MRI, CT, digital radiography, and in-house blood analyzers raise the baseline cost of every procedure
3. **Drug pricing** — Anesthesia, pain medication, and antibiotics have seen 30-50% price increases since 2020

### The marketing price trap

Here's what most clinics don't tell you: the price on their website is just the starting point. A "$40 spay" becomes $100-$240 after mandatory blood work, pain medication, and e-collar. A "$285 dental" becomes $895-$3,710 after extractions found mid-procedure.
