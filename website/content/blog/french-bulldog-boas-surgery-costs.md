If you own a French Bulldog, you already know: these loving, goofy companions come with a specific set of health challenges. **BOAS (Brachycephalic Obstructive Airway Syndrome)** is one of the most common and most serious.

BOAS surgery — also called nares resection, soft palate resection, or a combination of both — can be life-changing for your Frenchie. But in the U.S., it typically costs **$2,000 to $5,000**.

---

## What Is BOAS?

BOAS affects flat-faced (brachycephalic) breeds including French Bulldogs, English Bulldogs, Pugs, and Boston Terriers. These dogs have been bred for their shortened snouts, but this conformation causes:

- Stenotic nares (pinched nostrils)
- Elongated soft palate (tissue blocking the airway)
- Everted laryngeal saccules
- Narrowed trachea

**Signs your Frenchie may need BOAS surgery:**
- Loud snoring or noisy breathing
- Gagging or retching (especially in hot weather)
- Excessive panting
- Exercise intolerance
- Collapsing episodes after excitement or exercise
- Blue-tinged gums (severe cases — emergency)

## The Cost Problem

| Item | U.S. Cost |
|------|-----------|
| Pre-surgical workup (X-rays, bloodwork, airway exam) | $400 – $800 |
| BOAS surgery (nares + palate) | $1,500 – $3,500 |
| Anesthesia and monitoring | $300 – $700 |
| Post-op medications | $100 – $300 |
| **Total** | **$2,000 – $5,000** |

And for many Frenchie owners, this isn't a one-time cost. Many brachycephalic dogs also need:
- Orthopedic evaluation (prone to IVDD)
- Dental surgery (crowded teeth)
- Soft palate revision (if initial surgery doesn't fully resolve symptoms)
