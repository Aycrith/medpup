## What You Should NEVER Compromise On

A safe spay/neuter must include:

1. **Pre-anesthetic bloodwork** — detects hidden health issues
2. **IV catheter and fluids** — maintains blood pressure during surgery
3. **Endotracheal intubation** — protects the airway
4. **Pulse oximetry and ECG monitoring** — tracks heart and oxygen levels
5. **A dedicated monitoring technician** — someone watching only your pet
6. **Post-operative pain medication** — not optional
7. **E-collar (cone)** — prevents licking and infection

If a clinic doesn't offer these, ask why. If they don't offer IV fluids for a spay, walk away.
