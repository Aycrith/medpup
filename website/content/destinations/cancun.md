# Cancún, Mexico *(Archived: Phase 3)*

> Scope: this page documents the Cancún destination that was researched in Phase 0 and financial analysis in Phase 1. MedPup's active Phase 1 route is Pinellas → South Florida (ASPCA CVC + Good Care). Cancún is retained here for future reference only.

## Phase 3 Status

Cancún was the original Phase 0 primary hub — highest confidence surgical path — but Phase 1 margin analysis proved it does not work as a $900 concierge offering. The margin math is:

- Gross margin: -3% to +3% at $900 concierge fee
- Only self-selecting clients (who pay higher) can make this profitable
- APHIS/CDC paperwork, airfare cost, and boarding logistics add real friction

Cancún is kept as Phase 3 reference for:
- Clients who self-identify for surgical travel when domestic options are exhausted
- Future models where pricing adjusts to cover actual costs

## Archived — Veterinary Infrastructure

For complex procedures requiring advanced surgical expertise, Cancún offers board-certified surgeons, state-of-the-art facilities, and the same high standard of care found in top U.S. specialty hospitals — at a fraction of the cost.

**Why Cancún** *(archived analysis):*
- WHO Surgical Safety Checklist standards — same as U.S. teaching hospitals
- Board-certified veterinary surgeons — trained in U.S. and Europe
- Modern surgical suites — same equipment as top-tier specialty hospitals
- English-speaking medical staff throughout the surgical team
- Close proximity: direct flights from Miami in 45 minutes
