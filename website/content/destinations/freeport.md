# Freeport, Grand Bahama *(Archived: Phase 3)*

> Scope: this page documents the Freeport day-trip model researched in Phase 0. Freeport was considered the Phase 1 hub in the original Bahamas-first model but was superseded by the Pinellas → South Florida domestic route. Phase 1 margin math showed Freeport operating at +16% to +22% gross margin with potential cost overrun risk from ferry + APHIS fees. The domestic option requires none of this. Kept here for future reference.

## Phase 3 Status

Freeport has the better margin profile of the two international options researched. It is not included in Phase 1 because:
1. Phase 1 model (South Florida clinics) achieves $0 capital and 80-95% savings without travel
2. Freeport requires 3-hour ferry, departing at 7 AM, returning by 8 PM — a full-day commitment per client
3. APHIS paperwork and pre-trip health certificates add coordination complexity
4. The 22% gross margin at best is good — but Phase 1 delivers the same savings without the overhead

Freeport is retained as Phase 3 for:
- Clients who prefer a day-trip and avoid airfare costs
- Dental + soft tissue procedures where Freeport's CareCredit acceptance is a relevant advantage
- Future expansion once the Phase 1 domestic model stabilizes

## Archived — Freeport Infrastructure

Freeport is your entry point to affordable veterinary care. A 3-hour passenger ferry from Fort Lauderdale takes you and your pet to a fully accredited, English-speaking clinic where the same procedures cost 50-80% less than U.S. prices.

**Why Freeport** *(archived analysis):*
- **Hawksbill Creek Agreement** — the Freeport Free Trade Zone eliminates taxes until 2054, keeping clinic overhead low
- 3-hour ferry from Fort Lauderdale — pets in cabin, no cargo hold
- English-speaking staff throughout the surgical team
- Fully accredited surgical facility with dental, soft tissue, and orthopedic capabilities
- CareCredit accepted directly at the clinic
- WHO Surgical Safety Checklist protocols
