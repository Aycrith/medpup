When your dog needs surgery and the vet quotes $4,000-$6,000, your options feel limited. You can:

1. Put it on a credit card
2. Apply for CareCredit or Scratch Pay
3. Borrow from family
4. Surrender your pet
5. Find a way to reduce the cost

Option 5 is where MedPup can help — and it's often the best path.

### How CareCredit works

CareCredit is the largest veterinary healthcare financing card in the US, with over 12 million cardholders and 285,000+ enrolled locations. For vet expenses, it offers:

- 6, 12, 18, or 24-month deferred interest plans
- Minimum credit score around 600
- Works at over 100% of public vet universities

**The catch:** 54% of pet owners say they would have postponed care without CareCredit (CareCredit internal data, 2026). That means half of cardholders are choosing CareCredit because they have to, not because it's a great deal.

### Scratch Pay and other alternatives

Scratch Pay offers longer-term financing (up to 60 months) but requires a hard credit check and charges interest from day one. Other options like Pawvest and VetBilling offer similar terms.

### The real solution: reduce the cost

Every financing option carries interest or deferred interest that increases what you pay. The better approach is to reduce the total cost before you borrow.
