When your dog needs surgery, the first question is always: **how am I going to pay for this?**

With 53% of pet owners saying a $500-$2,500 vet bill is "impossible" to pay, and veterinary costs up 47% since 2020, financing is often the difference between getting your dog the care they need and being forced to delay or forgo treatment entirely.

Here are six options, ranked from most accessible to most specialized.

---

## 2. Pet Insurance (If Already Enrolled)

**Best for:** Pet owners who already have insurance with orthopedic/surgical coverage

Pet insurance penetration in the U.S. is only **4.6%**, so most families don't have this option. But if you do:

| Provider | Typical Reimbursement | Waiting Period |
|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Healthy Paws | 70-90% after deductible | 12 months for orthopedics |
| Trupanion | 90% unlimited | 30 days (ortho: 6 months for some) |
| Nationwide | 70-90% | 12 months for orthopedics |
| Embrace | 70-90% | 12 months for orthopedics |

**Important:** Most pet insurance policies have a 6-12 month waiting period before orthopedic coverage kicks in. If your dog was diagnosed before you enrolled, it's a pre-existing condition and won't be covered.

---

## 3. In-House Payment Plans

**Best for:** Families with an existing relationship with their vet

Some veterinary hospitals offer in-house payment plans, particularly for established clients. These are rare and becoming rarer — most vets have been burned by non-payment.

**What to ask:**
- "Do you offer any payment plan options for large procedures?"
- "Can I put down 50% now and pay the rest over 60 days?"
- "Is there a third-party financing option you work with?"

---

## 4. Scratchpay

**Best for:** Families who don't qualify for CareCredit

Scratchpay is a newer financing option specifically designed for veterinary care. Unlike CareCredit, it considers factors beyond just your credit score.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Credit score minimum | Lower than CareCredit |
| APR | Varies by term and creditworthiness |
| Terms | 3-36 months |
| Approval rate | Reportedly higher than CareCredit |

## 5. Veterinary School Hospitals

**Best for:** Families near a veterinary teaching hospital who can wait for an appointment

Veterinary teaching hospitals (University of Florida, University of Georgia, etc.) often charge 25-50% less than private specialty hospitals. The trade-off is longer wait times and procedures performed by residents under specialist supervision.

| Location | Distance from Miami |

| University of Florida (Gainesville) | ~5 hours drive |
| University of Georgia (Athens) | ~8 hours drive |

## What Not to Do

- **Don't use high-interest credit cards** (20%+ APR) for surgery you can't pay off quickly
- **Don't delay necessary surgery** while you figure out financing — some conditions worsen rapidly
- **Don't use human pain relievers** (ibuprofen, acetaminophen) — they're toxic to dogs
- **Don't choose anesthesia-free dental cleaning** — it's cosmetic only and misses disease below the gumline
