## The Risks of Doing It Yourself

Before MedPup's concierge service, pet owners who went to Mexico faced several challenges:

- **Veterinarian selection** — Google reviews are easy to fake; who do you actually trust?
- **Communication barriers** — medical terminology doesn't always translate well
- **Paperwork gaps** — Mexican health certificates for pet re-entry into the US must be completed correctly or your pet is denied boarding
- **Post-op complication management** — what happens if your pet needs follow-up care back in Florida?
- **No price guarantee** — you're a foreigner paying tourist prices; quotes can change

## What to Know Before Traveling

- **Consult your Florida vet first** — some conditions can't wait for travel
- **Allow 3–5 days in Cancun** — 1 day pre-op, surgery day, 1–3 days recovery before flying home
- **Not all pets are good candidates** — brachycephalic breeds (French Bulldogs, Pugs) face higher anesthesia risks and may not be suitable for travel
- **Check airline pet policies** — most US airlines allow in-cabin pets under 20 lbs; larger pets must fly in climate-controlled cargo
