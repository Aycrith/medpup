Medical tourism for pets is one of the fastest-growing trends in veterinary care — and it's easy to see why. With U.S. veterinary costs up **47% since 2020** and over **53% of pet owners** saying a $500-$2,500 bill is impossible to pay, crossing the border for affordable care has become a logical, proven option.

But it raises legitimate questions. Here are the 15 most common ones Florida pet owners ask.

---

### 1. Is pet medical tourism legal?

Yes. The USDA APHIS has clear regulations governing pet re-entry to the United States. As long as you comply with destination-specific requirements (health certificate, rabies vaccination, import permits), bringing your pet back into the U.S. is routine and legal. Over 36,000 dogs crossed from Mexico into the U.S. in 2025 alone.

### 2. Is the quality of care as good?

The same surgical techniques, implant manufacturers, and anesthesia protocols are used internationally. Many partner clinic surgeons are trained at U.S. or European veterinary schools and hold board certifications. The key is using a service that thoroughly vets its partner clinics — inspecting facilities, verifying credentials, and confirming safety protocols.

Absolutely. Your U.S. primary veterinarian provides the initial diagnosis, the pre-travel health certificate, and handles all post-operative follow-up. We provide them with a complete surgical report and the operating surgeon is available for a direct handoff call.
