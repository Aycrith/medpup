If your vet quoted you $1,500-$2,500 for dental cleaning with extractions, you're facing a common but frustrating reality: dental procedures are among the most expensive routine vet services in America. Here's what that money actually covers, and why the same procedure costs $285-$595 at MedPup partner clinics.

### What's included in a dental cleaning?

A proper veterinary dental cleaning includes:

- Pre-anesthetic blood work ($60-$120)
- General anesthesia with intubation ($50-$150)
- Digital dental X-rays ($50-$100)
- Supragingival and subgingival scaling ($50-$100)
- Polishing ($25-$50)
- Perio-probing and charting ($25-$50)
- Per-tooth extraction (if needed, $80-$250 per tooth)
- Pain medication ($25-$50)
- Anesthesia monitoring ($40-$80)

At a typical US vet, these add-ons bring the published marketing price of "$285 dental" to $895-$3,710 actual out-the-door.
