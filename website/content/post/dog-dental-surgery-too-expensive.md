Dental disease is the most common health problem in dogs, affecting **80% of dogs over age 3**. Left untreated, it causes chronic pain, infection, tooth loss, and can lead to heart, liver, and kidney damage.

Yet when your veterinarian recommends a dental cleaning with extractions, the estimate often lands between **$1,500 and $3,000** — a price that puts necessary care out of reach for many Florida families.

## Why Is Vet Dentistry So Expensive in Florida?

The cost breaks down roughly as follows:

| Item | Typical Cost |
|------|-------------|
| Pre-anesthetic bloodwork | $100 – $250 |
| Anesthesia monitoring | $200 – $500 |
| Dental cleaning & polishing | $300 – $800 |
| Full-mouth digital X-rays | $200 – $500 |
| Extractions (per tooth) | $50 – $150 |
| Pain medication | $50 – $150 |
| **Typical total** | **$1,500 – $3,000** |

The biggest cost drivers are anesthesia monitoring and the equipment overhead of a U.S. veterinary dental suite. These are legitimate costs — but they're also costs that can be dramatically lower at accredited clinics just 3 hours from Florida.
