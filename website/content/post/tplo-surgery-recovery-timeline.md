Your dog has been diagnosed with a torn ACL and you're facing a TPLO (Tibial Plateau Leveling Osteotomy) — the gold-standard surgery for cruciate ligament repair. Beyond the cost, the biggest concern most owners have is: **what does recovery look like?**

Here's an honest, week-by-week guide.

---

## The Recovery Timeline at a Glance

| Phase | Timeline | Key Activity |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Immediate post-op | Days 1-3 | Strict crate rest, pain management, ice packs |
| Early recovery | Week 1-2 | Leash walks only, incision care, suture removal |
| Mid recovery | Week 3-6 | Controlled leash walks, passive range of motion |
| Late recovery | Week 6-8 | Gradually increase leash walks, recheck X-rays |
| Full return | Week 8-12 | Gradual return to normal activity, rehab |

---

## Days 1-3: Immediate Post-Op

Your dog will be groggy, disoriented, and may whine or seem confused. This is normal.

**What to do:**
- Confine to a small area or crate. No freedom.
- Offer small amounts of water. If no vomiting after 2 hours, offer a small meal (half portion).
- Apply ice packs to the surgical site for 15 minutes, 3-4x daily for the first 48 hours.
- Give all medications exactly as prescribed. Set alarms if needed.
- **No licking or chewing** — e-collar must be on at all times.

**What to watch for:**
- Excessive swelling or discharge at incision
- Refusal to eat or drink for more than 24 hours
- Severe pain (panting, shaking, crying)
- Pale gums or difficulty breathing

**Call your vet if any of these occur.**

---

## Week 1-2: Early Recovery

The incision is healing and your dog is starting to feel better — which is actually dangerous. Dogs don't understand "rest."

**Activity rules:**
- **Leash walks only** for bathroom breaks. 5 minutes, 3-4x daily.
- No running, jumping, stairs, or rough play. Carry your dog up and down stairs.
- Crate or confined area when unsupervised. No exceptions.
- Suture/staple removal at your vet around day 10-14.

**Rehab:**
- Passive range of motion exercises: gently flex and extend the surgical leg 10 times, 3x daily.
- Gentle massage around (not on) the incision.

**Medications:** Continue pain meds and anti-inflammatories as prescribed. Don't stop early even if your dog seems fine.

---

## Week 3-4: Mid Recovery

Your dog is likely feeling much better. This is when most owners get complacent — and when most complications happen.

**Activity rules:**
- Leash walks: increase to 10 minutes, 3-4x daily.
- Still no running, jumping, or stairs.
- Still confined when unsupervised.

**Rehab:**
- Continue passive range of motion.
- Introduce controlled sits and downs.
- Balance exercises (standing on a pillow or soft surface for 10-15 seconds).

---

## Week 5-6: Building Strength

**Activity rules:**
- Leash walks: 15-20 minutes, 3x daily.
- Can begin short, slow trots on leash.
- Still no off-leash activity, dog parks, or rough play.

**Rehab:**
- Walking on gentle inclines (uphill builds quad strength).
- Backward walking for 10 steps, 3x daily.
- Cavaletti rails (walking over low obstacles).

---

## Week 6-8: Recheck and Progress

At week 6-8, your surgeon will take recheck X-rays to confirm bone healing. The bone healing determines when your dog can return to normal activity.

**If X-rays show good healing:**
- Gradually increase walk duration to 30 minutes.
- Begin introducing controlled off-leash activity in a secure area.
- Still no dog parks, fetch, or high-impact play.

**If X-rays show incomplete healing:**
- Continue restricted activity for another 2-4 weeks.
- Recheck again at week 10-12.

---

## Week 8-12: Return to Normal

**Activity rules:**
- Gradually return to normal activity over 2-4 weeks.
- Avoid high-impact activities (jumping for frisbees, agility) until week 12.
- Swimming is excellent low-impact exercise if the incision is fully healed.

**Long-term outlook:**
- 90-95% of dogs return to normal function.
- Arthritis may develop in the surgical knee over time — joint supplements and weight management help.
- The other knee has a 40-60% chance of tearing within 2-3 years.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Removing the e-collar too soon.** Leave it on for the full 10-14 days. One minute of licking can undo a week of healing.
2. **Letting your dog off leash at week 2.** They look fine — they're not. Bone healing takes 8+ weeks.
3. **Skipping pain medication.** Pain causes guarding, which delays recovery. Give meds on schedule.
4. **Not doing rehab exercises.** Passive range of motion and massage make a significant difference in recovery speed.
5. **Thinking "my dog is different."** They're not. Follow the protocol.
