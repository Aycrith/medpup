You've just been handed a $5,000 surgery estimate for your dog. You can't afford it. You feel like you're failing your best friend.

**You're not alone.** Veterinary costs have risen **47% since 2020**. Over **53% of pet owners** say a $500-$2,500 bill is impossible to pay. And over half of U.S. pet owners have delayed or skipped necessary veterinary care because of cost.

Here are 7 things you can do — starting today.

---

### 1. Get a Second Opinion

Not all vets charge the same. A second opinion from a different general practice vet (not a specialist) can sometimes be 20-40% lower. Call around to 3-4 clinics in your area and ask for a written estimate.

**What to ask:** "I have an estimate for [procedure] from my current vet. Could you give me a written quote for the same procedure?"

---

### 2. Ask About a Payment Plan

Some veterinary hospitals offer in-house payment plans, especially for established clients. You won't know unless you ask.

**Script:** "I'd love to proceed with the surgery my dog needs, but I can't afford the full amount upfront. Do you offer any payment plan options?"

Even a 50% deposit with the remainder over 30-60 days can make the difference.

### 4. Check Your Pet Insurance Coverage

If your dog is enrolled in pet insurance, check whether the procedure is covered. Even if you're outside the pre-existing condition window, some diagnostics (bloodwork, X-rays) may be covered even if surgery isn't.

**Key question:** "Does my plan cover cruciate ligament surgery / dental extractions / mass removal?" The answer varies dramatically by provider and plan level.

---

### 6. Explore Fundraising

Crowdfunding for veterinary care has become common and socially accepted:

- **GoFundMe** — Most popular. Share your dog's story and medical need.
- **Waggle** — Pet-specific crowdfunding platform
- **Social media** — Local pet groups on Facebook and Nextdoor are often surprisingly generous

**Tip:** Posts with photos, a specific dollar amount, and a clear timeline raise more money than vague requests.

## What Not to Do

🚫 **Don't use human pain relievers.** Ibuprofen, acetaminophen (Tylenol), and naproxen (Aleve) are toxic to dogs. A single tablet can cause kidney failure or internal bleeding.

🚫 **Don't try anesthesia-free dental cleaning.** It misses disease below the gumline, causes stress, and gives false reassurance. It's not a real alternative to professional cleaning.

🚫 **Don't wait too long.** Some conditions worsen rapidly. A $5,000 TPLO that's delayed can become a $10,000 problem with meniscal damage or arthritis.

🚫 **Don't surrender your dog without exploring all options.** Most shelters are overwhelmed. Rescue organizations exist specifically to help families keep their pets by funding necessary surgery.
