## The 4 Financing Options for Pet Surgery

### 1. CareCredit
A healthcare credit card accepted at many vet clinics.
- **The good:** 6–24 month deferred interest promotions
- **The trap:** If you miss the deadline by even one day, **all deferred interest (typically 26.99% APR) is charged retroactively** on the full original amount. Miss a $3,500 TPLO payment by 24 hours? You owe $950+ in interest.
- **Better option:** Ask for the extended payment plan (24–60 months at 14.99% fixed APR) — no retroactive interest, just a predictable monthly payment

### 2. Scratchpay
A veterinary-specific installment loan.
- **The good:** Soft credit check upfront, no deferred interest gimmicks
- **The trap:** High APRs for shorter terms (up to 39.99% on 6-month plans). What looks like "only $99/month" can mean you pay **2–3x the surgery cost** over the loan term
- **Better option:** Choose the longest term available (36–60 months) to keep monthly payments manageable, then pay early with no prepayment penalty

### 3. In-Clinic Payment Plans
Some clinics offer internal financing.
- **The good:** No credit check, flexible terms
- **The trap:** These are often informal and poorly documented. We've seen clinics add "administration fees," "late payment processing," and "account maintenance charges" — sometimes totaling **$200–$500 in extra fees** on a $3,000 surgery
- **Better option:** Get everything in writing before signing. Ask for a total finance charge expressed as a dollar amount, not a percentage

### 4. Personal Loans and Credit Cards
- **The good:** Potentially lower APRs (10–22%) for borrowers with good credit
- **The trap:** If your credit isn't excellent, you're looking at **25–36% APR**. And once the money hits your bank account, it's tempting to use it for other things
- **Better option:** Use a 0% APR balance transfer card (12–18 month intro period) — pay the surgery within the promo window and owe $0 in interest

## What Nobody Tells You About Hidden Add-Ons

This is the part that financing ads don't mention. Even after you've "financed" the surgery, many clinics add:

- **Emergency overnight monitoring** ($400–$800/night) — "recommended" but usually not included in the quote
- **Specialty implant sizes** ($200–$600) — TPLO plate sizes vary and the "standard" quote covers the smallest plate
- **Extended hospitalization** ($150–$300/day) — recovery takes longer than expected
- **Post-op imaging** ($150–$400) — follow-up x-rays to confirm healing
- **Recheck exams** ($50–$150 per visit) — two to three follow-ups is routine
- **Medication refills** ($25–$75 each) — pain meds, antibiotics, sedatives

A $3,500 TPLO quote can easily become **$5,200+** with "recommended" add-ons. If you financed the original $3,500, you're now making payments on a surgery that's incomplete.
