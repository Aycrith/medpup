## What's Actually Included in a Dog Dental Cleaning

The base price often only covers the procedure room and the technician's time. When you compare quotes, ask what's included:

- **Anesthesia** — essential for a safe, thorough cleaning ($150–$350)
- **Oral exam and charting** — full periodontal probing ($50–$100)
- **Ultrasonic scaling and polishing** — the actual cleaning ($100–$250)
- **Dental x-rays** — the single most important diagnostic tool ($100–$300)
- **Pre-anesthetic bloodwork** — safety screening ($75–$200)
- **IV catheter and fluids** — standard of care ($50–$150)
- **Pain medication** — post-procedure comfort ($25–$75)
- **Antibiotics** — if infection is present ($20–$60)

A *real* all-in price for a comprehensive dental cleaning with x-rays is **$500–$800** at most Tampa Bay general practices. Specialty hospitals and emergency clinics routinely charge **$900–$1,200+**. Low-cost clinics may advertise $199 but exclude anesthesia monitoring, x-rays, and pain management.

## Why Tampa Bay Vet Costs Keep Rising

Veterinary costs have climbed **60%+ since 2014**, and Tampa Bay has felt it acutely. Factors include:

- **Consolidation** — large corporate groups now own many local practices, increasing standard prices
- **Staffing shortages** — veterinary technician wages have risen sharply
- **Equipment upgrades** — digital dental x-ray and anesthesia monitoring are now standard of care
- **Real estate costs** — Pinellas County commercial rents are among Florida's highest

## Signs Your Dog Needs a Dental Cleaning

- Bad breath that doesn't resolve with brushing
- Yellow or brown tartar buildup on teeth
- Red, swollen, or bleeding gums
- Reluctance to eat hard food or chew toys
- Pawing at the mouth or drooling excessively
