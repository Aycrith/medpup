## Part 5: Strategic Implications

### Why Cancún Wins on Surgical Certainty

The Cancún clinics (VetCancun, CAP Cancún) provide explicit, documented evidence of the complete service suite the business model requires: orthopedic surgery (confirmed specialty at CAP), dental procedures (both list dental), and travel certificates for US return (VetCancun explicitly lists USDA). **Neither of these service categories is explicitly confirmed** at either Freeport GB clinic from any public source.

Cancún additionally has a proven market: 36,670+ dogs crossed into Mexico in 2025, with an established industry (MexiVet Express, PPI Mexico, AAPSO) operating across multiple border points and air routes. The MedPup concept in Cancún would be entering a validated ecosystem.

### Why Freeport Retains Strategic Attraction

Freeport has zero direct competition (no other Florida-Freeport pet concierge), zero screwworm friction (Bahamas not on screwworm list), and a verified ferry link with verified pet accommodation. The regulatory framework is manageable (Click2Clear ePermitting, 24–48h, $50), no screwworm cert, no screwworm-affected-country status, and no requirement for BAHFSA health-cert endorsement, making the cross-border regulatory skeleton functionally less complex than Mexico's.

The critical path problem remains: verifying whether Freeport GB contains at least one clinic with confirmed orthopedic and dental surgical capability — via either direct confirmation (phone) or establishment of a Nassau-based rotating-clinic model.

### Hybrid Scenario: Nassau Pet Clinic Mobile Visit

The Nassau Pet Clinic mobile service ("high-tech state-of-the-art mobile veterinary hospital" that "performs surgeries and dental procedures") introduces a third model: a rotating Nassau-based surgical visit to Freeport, eliminating the Freeport capability gap entirely without the Cancún air-travel overhead. Its feasibility hinges on verifying mobile visit pricing, logistics, and owner-approval sub-regulatory requirements.
