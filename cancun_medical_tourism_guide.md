### 2.2 Mexico SENASICA Import Requirements (US → Mexico)

| Requirement | Detail | Cost |
|-------------|--------|------|
| **Microchip** | ISO 11784/11785 compliant 15-digit microchip | $25–$50 (if needed) |
| **Rabies vaccine** | ≥21 days before entry (primary); booster valid immediately. Microchip must pre-date vaccine | $15–$25 |
| **Health certificate (CVP-70)** | Issued within **10 days** of departure. Signed by USDA-accredited US vet. Format: bilingual acceptable | $50–$100 (vet exam + paperwork) |
| **Screwworm certificate** | **Mandatory since Nov 2024**. Treatment (ivermectin) within 5 days of travel. Vet inspection certifying freedom from screwworm | $10–$30 (treatment) + $20–$50 (cert paperwork) |
| **APHIS endorsement** | USDA APHIS VS must endorse the HC. By mail: 1–2 wks ($38–$50 + shipping). Electronically via VEHCS: 1–3 days | $38–$50 |
| **Import permit** | Not needed for tourist stays <60 days | $0 |
| **Total prep cost per pet** | Vet exam + HC + screwworm + APHIS endorsement | **$100–$225** |

**Timeline to prepare:** 3–4 weeks recommended:
1. Week 1: Ensure microchip is in, rabies vaccine is current
2. Week 3: Schedule vet appointment (within 10 days of travel)
3. Week 3 (within 5 days of travel): Screwworm treatment
4. Submit to APHIS: Mail (2 wks) or VEHCS (1–3 days)
5. Book airline pet reservation

### 2.3 US Return Leg — Documentation (Mexico → US)

| Requirement | Detail | Cost |
|-------------|--------|------|
| **Option A: Single US-issued HC covers both legs** | If total trip is <10 days, the US issued APHIS-endorsed health certificate covers both arrival to Mexico AND return to US | Included in $100–$225 above |
| **Option B: Mexican vet issues return HC** | Mexican veterinarian issues a new HC within 10 days of return flight. Must be in Spanish/English | $35–$100 |
| **Option B endorsement** | SENASICA (Mexico's USDA equivalent) endorsement — Cancun office can do same-day | $20–$40 |
| **CDC requirements** | Mexico is NOT a high-risk rabies country per CDC. Standard re-entry. | $0 |
| **CBP requirements** | Visual inspection. CBP agriculture specialist not typically needed for Mexico returning dogs | $0 |

**Best practice for MedPup:** Plan trips of 7–10 days maximum so the US-issued health certificate covers both legs. This eliminates the need for Mexican vet involvement in documentation on the return side, simplifying the pipeline.

### 3.2 Concierge Fee Targets

| Procedure tier | Client US cost | Cancún cost + concierge | Max concierge fee for 30% savings |
|---------------|---------------|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Dental cleaning** | $500–$1,200 | $645–$1,220 + fee | $700–$1,000 (30% savings) |
| **Spay/neuter** | $300–$600 | $645–$1,220 + fee | $600–$750 (marginal) |
| **Mass removal** | $1,000–$3,000 | $645–$1,220 + fee | $1,000–$1,500 (40% savings) |
| **FHO / fracture** | $2,000–$5,000 | $1,080–$1,975 + fee | $1,500–$2,500 (30–50% savings) |

**Key insight:** Low-complexity procedures (dental cleanings, spay/neuter) at Cancún show marginal savings when you add air travel + concierge fee. The model only makes compelling financial sense for **high-complexity procedures** (mass removal, orthopedic surgery) where US costs are $2,000+.
