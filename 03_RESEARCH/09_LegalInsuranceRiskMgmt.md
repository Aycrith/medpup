## 9.1 VETERINARY TELEMEDICINE & REFERRAL LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### Key Principle: MedPup Does Not Practice Veterinary Medicine

MedPup's legal safety rests on a clear delineation: it is a **logistics and concierge service**, not a veterinary practice. This distinction is critical for avoiding unauthorized practice of veterinary medicine claims.

### Veterinary-Client-Patient Relationship (VCPR)

Under AVMA guidelines and most state veterinary practice acts:
- A VCPR must exist between the pet owner, the pet, and a licensed veterinarian for that vet to diagnose, prescribe, or treat.
- MedPup **does not establish a VCPR** — it connects clients with licensed veterinarians who establish their own VCPR.
- The Client Service Agreement (Phase 3) explicitly states MedPup is not a veterinary practice.

### Referral Fee Compliance

- **Kickback concerns:** Paying veterinarians for referrals can violate state anti-kickback laws and AVMA ethics guidelines.
- **MedPup's approach:** Pays a "Post-Operative Follow-Up Fee" for **actual clinical services rendered** (the 14-day recheck exam), not for the referral itself. This is a fee-for-service model, not a kickback.
- **Legal precedent:** Medical tourism facilitators in human healthcare use similar models — paying for actual services, not referrals.

### State-by-State Veterinary Practice Act Variability

Florida Statute 474 regulates veterinary medicine. Key considerations:
- MedPup does not diagnose, prescribe, or treat — staying outside the scope of regulated activity.
- The concierge may handle paperwork and logistics but must not provide medical advice.
- If the concierge is a licensed veterinary technician, they must be careful not to exceed their scope under Florida law.

## 9.4 LIABILITY WAIVER LEGAL LANGUAGE

The Client Service Agreement (Phase 3) includes:
- **Assumption of risk:** Client acknowledges travel, anesthesia, and surgery carry inherent risks.
- **Release of MedPup:** For claims arising from clinic care (which MedPup does not control).
- **Emergency authorization:** Concierge may authorize emergency treatment if client unreachable.
- **Governing law:** Florida.

These provisions should be reviewed by a Florida-licensed attorney before the first trip. Estimated cost: $500-$1,500 for a one-time contract review.

|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| USDA APHIS VS | Pet import/export rules, health certificate requirements | Weekly check |
| CDC | Dog importation rules, rabies country classifications | Monthly check |
| CBP | Port-of-entry procedures, screwworm program updates | Monthly check |

| Bahamas Dept. of Agriculture | Click2Clear system, import permit requirements | Before each trip |

### Key Regulatory Changes to Watch

1. **CDC dog import rule revisions** (expected 2025-2026): May affect rabies vaccination documentation requirements.
2. **Screwworm status changes** (Mexico): If Mexico regains screwworm-free status, the certificate requirement could be lifted, simplifying Cancun trips.
3. **Florida veterinary practice act amendments**: Any changes to telemedicine or referral rules could affect MedPup's positioning.

## 9.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP COMPLIANCE

The single most important legal principle: **MedPup is a travel logistics company that happens to serve pet owners seeking veterinary care, not a veterinary company that arranges travel.** This framing, consistently maintained in all communications and agreements, is the shield against regulatory risk.
