## 12.2 CORPORATE CONSOLIDATION TRENDS

### Private Equity in Veterinary Medicine

Private equity firms have been aggressively acquiring veterinary practices since ~2017. This trend is driving up consumer prices.

| Consolidator | Parent/Backer | Practices Owned (est.) |
|-------------|---------------|------------------------|
| **Mars Veterinary Health** | Mars, Inc. (family-owned) | 2,500+ (Banfield, VCA, BluePearl, Linnaeus, AniCura) |
| **National Veterinary Associates (NVA)** | JAB Holding Company | 1,400+ |
| **Pathway Vet Alliance** | TSG Consumer Partners | 400+ |
| **PetVet Care Centers** | Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan | 400+ |
| **Southern Veterinary Partners** | Shore Capital Partners | 300+ |
| **VetCor** | Oak Hill Capital Partners | 400+ |

**Source:** VIN News, The Atlantic, industry analyst reports.

### Consolidation Impact on Pricing

- Corporate-owned practices charge **10-30% more** on average for equivalent procedures compared to independently-owned practices.
- PE-backed consolidation typically results in: higher prices, standardized treatment protocols (upselling), reduction in non-revenue-generating services, and pressure on veterinarians to meet production targets.
- The Atlantic (2025) documented these trends: "Private equity's involvement in the industry has been great for corporate bottom lines and not so great for veterinarians or consumers."

### The Mars Veterinary Health Ecosystem

Mars owns the full vertical: Banfield (general practice, 1,000+ locations in PetSmart), VCA (general + specialty, 1,000+ hospitals), and BluePearl (specialty/emergency, 100+ hospitals). Together, they control a significant share of the U.S. small animal veterinary market.

### Veterinarian Burnout

- 41% of veterinarians report experiencing financial euthanasia at their practice at least sometimes
- 74% say it is among the hardest aspects of practice
- 48% received NO formal training on discussing cost with clients
- 32% feel "minimally prepared" for cost conversations

**Source:** PetSmart Charities-Gallup State of Pet Care Study (2025).

## 12.5 PHARMACEUTICAL PRICING DYNAMICS

### Markup Chains

U.S. veterinary pharmaceuticals pass through multiple markup layers:
1. Manufacturer → Distributor: 10-15% markup
2. Distributor → Veterinary Clinic: 15-30% markup
3. Clinic → Pet Owner: 100-200% markup

The same medication (e.g., insulin, gabapentin, carprofen) may cost **2-5x more** through a U.S. veterinary clinic than through a Mexican pharmacy — even when manufactured by the same company at the same facility.

### Insulin Case Study

| Product | U.S. Pharmacy Price | Mexico Pharmacy Price | Savings |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|---------|
| Lantus (glargine) 10mL vial | $300-$500 | $40-$60 | 85-90% |
| Novolin N 10mL vial | $100-$150 | $15-$25 | 80-85% |
| Vetsulin (porcine insulin) | $50-$80 | Not widely available | — |

**Source:** Cross-referenced GoodRx, SingleCare, and Mexico pharmacy price surveys; consistent with Phase 1 Lab Grade Insulin research.

## 12.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP POSITIONING

1. **The industry structure itself creates MedPup's opportunity:** Corporate consolidation drives up prices, the workforce shortage creates access barriers, and the pharmaceutical markup chain amplifies costs. These are structural, not cyclical problems — they will not self-correct.
2. **The veterinary shortage means long waits even for those who can pay:** A dog needing TPLO may face 2-6 month wait at a university hospital. MedPup offers immediate scheduling.
3. **Veterinarians are stressed and under-supported in cost conversations:** MedPup's vet referral program relieves this burden — the vet gets an answer to "What can I do if I can't afford this?" that isn't euthanasia.
4. **The drug price differential supports a medication-only trip product:** Even without surgery, a Freeport medication pickup trip for expensive chronic medications (insulin, heart medications, seizure medications) could serve a large market.
