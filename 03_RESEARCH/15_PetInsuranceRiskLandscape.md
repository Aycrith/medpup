## 15.4 COVERAGE GAPS FOR PET CARE BUSINESSES

The Triple-I specifically warned about coverage gaps relevant to MedPup's concierge model:

- Pet sitters, dog walkers, and in-home pet care providers may face significant coverage gaps if relying solely on homeowners, renters, or standard general liability policies.
- Standard policies often exclude business-related activities conducted in the home and damage involving animals under a business's care, custody, or control.
- Specialized pet business liability coverage, endorsements, or inland marine coverage may be necessary depending on services provided.[reference:7]

### MedPup Insurance Implication
The concierge model — where MedPup staff take physical custody of client pets during ferry transit and clinic stays — falls squarely into the "care, custody, or control" category that standard policies exclude. Specialized **bailee's coverage** is essential.

## 15.5 THE 96% UNINSURED: MEDPUP'S ADDRESSABLE MARKET

With fewer than 4% of U.S. pets insured, approximately **96% of U.S. pets have no insurance coverage**. This means:

- For a TPLO surgery at $5,200 (VCA published pricing), 96% of dog owners pay entirely out of pocket.
- For dental extractions at $2,500-$3,200, 96% pay out of pocket.
- Even among the ~4% insured, annual maximums (typically $5,000-$10,000) and pre-existing condition exclusions limit coverage for major procedures.

## 15.7 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **96% uninsured = massive out-of-pocket market.** The vast majority of MedPup's target clients have no insurance buffer against U.S. surgical pricing. This amplifies the value of MedPup's cost reduction.
2. **Insurance is growing fast but from a tiny base.** At 20.9% annual growth, insured pets will double in ~3.5 years. MedPup should build insurance navigation into its service model now.
3. **Liability coverage is non-negotiable.** The concierge model creates "care, custody, control" exposure that standard business insurance excludes. Specialized coverage must be in place before Trip 1.
4. **The gap between premiums ($5.47B) and claims ($1.86B for dog injuries alone) suggests a healthy insurance market with room for innovation.** Pet insurers may become MedPup partners, not adversaries, as they seek to manage costs through international care networks.
