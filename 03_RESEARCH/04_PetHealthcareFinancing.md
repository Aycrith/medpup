CareCredit, owned by Synchrony Financial, is the largest pet healthcare financing vehicle in the United States.

**Sources:** CareCredit's provider page states acceptance in 75% of vet hospitals (~25,000+ locations) with cardholders using the card 5.5x annually for vet services. Approximately 5,700 new cardholders are approved daily[reference:54]. Forbes reports 12 million cardholders and 270,000 participating providers in 2024, up from 4.4 million cardholders a decade prior[reference:55]. The broader medical patient-financing industry generated $15.3 billion in revenue in 2023[reference:56].

### The CareCredit Reality

While CareCredit provides access to financing, it does not reduce the cost of care — it simply converts an unaffordable lump sum into monthly payments, often with deferred interest provisions that can trap consumers. The CFPB has raised concerns that "the growing promotion and use of medical cards and installment loans can increase the financial burden on patients"[reference:57].

**For MedPup:** CareCredit acceptance at the Freeport partner clinic is a differentiator. The client gets the **double benefit** of a dramatically lower procedure cost AND the ability to finance it.

**Sources:** AVMA reports U.S. pet insurance industry surpassed $4.7 billion in 2024, with 6.4 million pets insured at year-end. Market penetration reached 3.9% overall — 5.5% for dogs and 2.0% for cats[reference:58]. The North American pet health insurance market reached $5.2 billion in written premium with 7.03 million insured pets, a 20.9% year-over-year increase[reference:59].

### Key Implication

**~94.5% of U.S. dogs have no insurance.** This means the vast majority of dog owners are paying 100% out of pocket for veterinary surgery. When a TPLO costs $5,200 at VCA, that is a direct hit to household finances for 94.5% of owners. These owners are MedPup's addressable market.

Even among the 5.5% of insured dogs, coverage may be limited by:
- Annual maximums (often $5,000–$10,000)
- Pre-existing condition exclusions (CCL tears in the opposite leg are common)
- Reimbursement models (owner pays upfront, then claims)

## 4.3 THE $55,000 LIFETIME COST

CareCredit's own research identifies the cost of care as the **#1 barrier** to pet treatment, with the average pet's lifetime veterinary cost estimated at **$55,000** over a 15-year lifespan[reference:60].

A single TPLO at $5,200 consumes ~9.5% of the total lifetime budget in one event. Dental extractions at $2,500 consume ~4.5%. For households with multiple dogs, these percentages multiply.

## 4.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP POSITIONING

The pet healthcare financing market is large ($15.3 billion in patient financing alone) but fundamentally flawed: it enables payment of inflated U.S. prices rather than addressing the price itself.
