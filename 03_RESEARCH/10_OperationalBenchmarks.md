## 10.1 CROSS-BORDER VETERINARY WORKFLOW STANDARDS

### WHO Surgical Safety Checklist (Veterinary Adaptation)

The World Health Organization's Surgical Safety Checklist has been adapted for veterinary use. MedPup requires partner clinics to use it. The checklist includes:

| Phase | Items |
|-------|-------|
| **Before induction of anesthesia** | Patient ID verified, procedure site marked, anesthesia machine check, known allergies documented, airway/aspiration risk assessed |
| **Before skin incision** | Team introductions, anticipated critical events, antibiotic prophylaxis confirmed, imaging displayed |
| **Before patient leaves OR** | Instrument/sponge count, specimen labeling, equipment issues, recovery and pain management plan |

### Standard of Care Indicators for Partner Clinics

| Indicator | Minimum Standard | Verification Method |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Pre-anesthetic bloodwork | Required for all surgical patients >5 years old | Check clinic protocol; review sample records |
| IV catheter placement | Required for all general anesthesia cases | Observe during audit |
| Continuous monitoring | ECG, SpO2, blood pressure, temperature | Equipment check during audit |
| Dedicated anesthesia technician | One technician per patient, not shared with surgery | Observe staffing during audit |
| Post-operative pain scoring | Documented pain assessment every 4 hours | Review sample discharge records |
| Written discharge instructions | Provided to every client, in English | Review sample |

## 10.2 PET TRANSPORT BEST PRACTICES

### Ferry Transport (Freeport)

- **Carrier requirements:** IATA-compliant carriers for small dogs; secure leash/harness for large dogs
- **Temperature monitoring:** Avoid enclosed vehicle holds; keep pets in climate-controlled passenger areas
- **Relief breaks:** Use designated pet relief areas on ferry; carry waste bags
- **Motion sickness:** Most dogs do not experience ferry motion sickness; have cleaning supplies on hand

### Air Transport (Cancun)

- **Cabin vs. cargo:** All MedPup pets travel in-cabin where airline permits; otherwise, temperature-controlled cargo
- **Airline pet policies:** American Airlines allows small pets in cabin ($125 each way); larger pets travel in cargo. Spirit Airlines allows small pets in cabin only.
- **Documentation:** Airlines require health certificates issued within 10 days of travel (domestic) or per destination country rules (international)
- **Sedation policy:** Most veterinary organizations advise against sedating pets for air travel due to respiratory risks at altitude

## 10.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP OPERATIONS

**The concierge model is the moat.** While competitors can replicate the logistics (booking ferries, filing permits), the in-person, empathetic, owner-accompanied experience is harder to copy at scale. Investing in concierge training, client communication standards, and experience consistency will be the primary competitive advantage as the market grows.
