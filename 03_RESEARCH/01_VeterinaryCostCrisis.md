## 1.2 THE INFLATION: How Fast Costs Are Rising

- **Decade trend:** Veterinary care costs have risen **60%** over the past 10 years (Bureau of Labor Statistics data analyzed by Morning Brew)[reference:7].
- **Recent acceleration:** Urban veterinary services saw a **5.9%** price increase from March 2024 to March 2025 — more than **double** the 2.4% average rise in all consumer goods[reference:8].
- **One-year comparison:** In the past year alone, vet care costs rose **2.5 times** the rate of everyday items like food or gas[reference:9].
- **30-year perspective:** U.S. veterinary service prices have surged approximately **300%** over the last three decades, driving the cross-border trend[reference:10].
- **Total spending:** Americans spent approximately **$38 billion** on pet healthcare in 2023, up from $29 billion in 2019 — a 31% increase in four years[reference:11].

## 1.3 THE HUMAN COST: Economic Euthanasia

The term "economic euthanasia" describes the euthanasia of pets with treatable conditions solely because the owner cannot afford care. This is not a fringe phenomenon — it is a systemic crisis.

| Metric | Data Point | Source |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Pets euthanized annually due to financial constraints | ~**66% (2 of every 3)** of all euthanasias | Multiple studies via Treasured Pets Foundation |
| Pet guardians who would consider euthanasia if faced with costly treatment | **48% (1 in 2)** | Same source |
| U.S. households that have **skipped or declined** veterinary care due to cost | **52%** of 87 million pet-owning households | Gallup poll (2024) |
| Pet owners who forgo care for financial reasons | **7 in 10** | Same Gallup data |
| Australian parallel (comparable market) | **4 out of 5** believe $3,000 is too much to pay | PetSure (2024) |

**Sources:** Treasured Pets Foundation analysis citing multiple JAVMA studies: 66% of euthanasias are economic, and 48% of guardians would consider it[reference:12]. Gallup polling finds 52% of U.S. pet owners have put off veterinary care because of cost, with 7 in 10 citing financial reasons[reference:13]. An Australian study found that 4 out of 5 pet owners consider $3,000 as the breaking point[reference:14].

### Case Study: The Harris Family (Georgia)

Sofia, an 8-year-old 8-pound dog, was diagnosed with a bacterial infection. Five different vet clinics quoted the family thousands of dollars. The choice was between economic euthanasia or impossible debt. "It's like they're bullying you to pay their price, and you have to make a choice you don't want to make"[reference:15].

### Case Study: Gus the Dachshund (Oregon)

Gus needed IVDD spinal surgery. The Portland quote: $12,000. His owners, Thomas and Loralee Kitts, "were at a loss ... wondering, what the hell should we do? This is our little guy." They discovered Vet Voyage, a company flying pets to Mexico City for surgery, and avoided euthanasia[reference:16].

## 1.4 THE SUPPLY SIDE: Veterinary Specialist Shortage

The cost problem is compounded by an access problem.

- **National shortage:** The U.S. is experiencing a nationwide veterinary shortage, with shelters and nonprofit clinics hit hardest[reference:17].
- **Wait times:** 79% of nonprofit clinics reported longer-than-usual wait times, with **51% experiencing delays of 2 months or more**, and some as long as **6 months**[reference:18].
- **Shelter impact:** 73% of animal sheltering organizations report being short-staffed for veterinarians. 91% have spay/neuter backlogs[reference:19].
- **Unperformed surgeries:** An estimated **2.7 million** spay/neuter surgeries were never performed due to the shortage[reference:20].
- **Private equity consolidation:** Private equity firms have been acquiring independent veterinary practices, shifting to for-profit models that drive up prices[reference:21].

## 1.5 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP POSITIONING

The U.S. veterinary market has a structural gap:

1. **Demand is high:** 89.7 million dogs in the U.S. (AVMA 2024 data), with 59.8 million dog-owning households[reference:22][reference:23].
2. **Prices are rising unsustainably fast:** 5.9% annual inflation in vet services vs. 2.4% for all goods.
3. **Insurance penetration is minimal:** Only ~5.5% of dogs are insured, meaning 94.5% of owners pay out of pocket[reference:24].
4. **Financing exists but is debt:** CareCredit has 12 million cardholders, but this represents households taking on high-interest medical debt, not a sustainable affordability solution[reference:25].
5. **The emotional toll is real:** When 48% of owners would consider euthanasia over a large vet bill, the market is crying out for a legitimate cost-reduction pathway.
