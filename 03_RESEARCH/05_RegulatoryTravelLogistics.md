## 5.1 USDA APHIS PET IMPORT/EXPORT FRAMEWORK

### Agency Overview

The USDA Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS) regulates the import and export of pets. In 2024, APHIS:
- Issued 2,295 import permits covering 6,089 dogs for resale in the United States
- Conducted over 9,700 site inspections (including 1,200+ unannounced)
- Found 96% of licensees and registrants in substantial compliance

**Source:** APHIS 2024 Impact Report[reference:63][reference:64].

### Bahamas Export Requirements (U.S. → Bahamas)

- Valid USDA-accredited health certificate issued within 10 days of travel
- Current rabies vaccination (at least 30 days old, not expired)
- Bahamas Department of Agriculture import permit via Click2Clear system ($11.20 per pet)

### U.S. Re-Entry from Bahamas

- USDA health certificate (endorsed or accredited)
- Valid rabies certificate
- Visual inspection by CBP at Port Everglades
- **No screwworm certificate required for Bahamas** (unlike Mexico)

### U.S. Re-Entry from Mexico

- USDA health certificate
- Rabies certificate
- **Screwworm inspection certificate** (mandatory as of Nov 22, 2024) — must be issued within 5 days of return by a SENASICA-authorized Mexican veterinarian[reference:65]

### August 1, 2024 Rule Changes

The CDC changed dog import rules effective August 1, 2024. Key changes affect dogs coming from countries considered high-risk for rabies. The Bahamas is not a high-risk country; Mexico's status should be verified before each trip. The AKC reported in October 2025 that proposed CDC revisions to import requirements are delayed due to federal workforce reductions[reference:66].

## 5.2 BALEARIA CARIBBEAN FERRY: FORT LAUDERDALE ↔ FREEPORT

### Route Details

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Operator | Balearia Caribbean |
| Route | Fort Lauderdale Cruise Terminal → Freeport Harbour |
| Crossing time | ~3 hours |
| Frequency | 2x per week (varies seasonally) |
| Typical schedule | Departs 9:00 AM, returns ~5:30 PM (Wed & Sun per recent data) |
| Fare (round trip) | ~$244–$254 per person |
| Departure port | Port Everglades, Terminal 21 |

**Sources:** Rome2Rio confirms Balearia Caribbean operates the Fort Lauderdale to Freeport ferry with 3-hour crossing time and fares of approximately $244–$254[reference:67]. Schedule data indicates Wednesday and Sunday departures with 9:00 AM outbound and ~5:30 PM return[reference:68].

### Operational Note

The 9:00 AM departure and ~5:30 PM return create a tighter surgical window than the 7:00 AM departure used in MedPup's pilot itinerary. **Verify current schedule with Balearia Caribbean before booking each trip**, as schedules change seasonally. If the 9:00 AM schedule is the only option, the clinic day shortens to approximately 11:30 AM – 4:00 PM, which may limit the number of procedures per trip.

## 5.3 MEXICO-SPECIFIC REGULATORY ADDENDUM (FOR FUTURE CANCUN TRIPS)

### SENASICA Authorization

Mexican veterinary clinics performing screwworm inspections for U.S. re-entry must have a veterinarian authorized by SENASICA (Servicio Nacional de Sanidad, Inocuidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria). This is a critical credential to verify during the partner clinic inspection process.

### Cédula Profesional Verification

All Mexican veterinary surgeons should have:
- **Cédula Profesional** (federal professional license) — verifiable via the SEP (Secretaría de Educación Pública) online database
- **Cédula de Especialidad** (specialty certification, if applicable) — for orthopedics, oncology, ophthalmology

### COFEPRIS Registration

The clinic itself should have COFEPRIS sanitary registration (Comisión Federal para la Protección contra Riesgos Sanitarios), equivalent to state-level health department licensing in the U.S.

## 5.4 TRAVEL INSURANCE CONSIDERATIONS

MedPup should recommend (but not require) that clients consider:
1. **Trip cancellation insurance** — covers concierge fee if illness/weather prevents travel
2. **Pet travel insurance** — some providers offer specific coverage for pets traveling internationally
3. **Personal travel insurance** — for the human client; standard travel policies may exclude pet-related claims

## 5.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP OPERATIONS

The regulatory environment for cross-border pet travel is complex but navigable. The Bahamas route (Freeport) is significantly simpler than the Mexico route (Cancun) because:
1. No screwworm certificate required.
2. Shorter travel time (3-hour ferry vs. flight logistics).
3. CBP re-entry is routine for ferry passengers from the Bahamas.
4. August 2024 CDC rule changes do not affect Bahamas-sourced returns.

**The Freeport pilot is the logical entry point.** Cancun should follow only after the Freeport model is proven and operational learnings are incorporated.
