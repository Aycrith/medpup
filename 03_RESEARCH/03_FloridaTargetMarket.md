- Dog ownership has declined from 42% in 2018 to 38% in 2024, partly due to the housing affordability crisis[reference:48].

- Higher-income households and married couples with children have the highest ownership rates[reference:50].

## 3.3 FLORIDA COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE: Specialty Veterinary Costs

### BluePearl Specialty Hospitals (Multiple Florida Locations)

BluePearl is one of the largest specialty/emergency chains in Florida, with locations in Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Tampa, Orlando, and beyond. Their pricing represents the "unaffordable" baseline that drives clients to MedPup.

| Procedure | Typical BluePearl / Specialty Quote |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| TPLO | $4,500–$5,500 |
| IVDD spinal surgery | $8,000–$12,000 |
| Dental with extractions | $1,500–$3,200 |
| Mass removal (specialty) | $2,500–$5,000 |
| BOAS airway surgery | $3,000–$5,000 |

### University of Florida Veterinary Hospital (Gainesville)

UF is Florida's flagship veterinary teaching hospital. While sometimes slightly less expensive than private specialty, wait times can extend months for non-emergency surgeries — consistent with the national shortage data showing 51% of clinics with 2+ month delays[reference:51].

## 3.4 TARGET CLIENT PROFILE

### Primary Target: The "Sticker Shock" Dog Owner

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Location | Within 2-hour drive of Fort Lauderdale (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach) |
| Situation | Just received a specialist quote they cannot afford |
| Emotion | Guilt, desperation, love — willing to explore alternatives |
| Income | $50,000–$120,000 household (too much for assistance, too little for $5,000 surgery) |
| Pet insurance | Most likely **uninsured** (94.5% of U.S. dogs are uninsured)[reference:52] |
| Research behavior | Googles "affordable [procedure] for my dog" or "cheap TPLO Florida" |
| Decision trigger | Quote exceeds $3,000 (the psychological breakpoint)[reference:53] |

### Secondary Target: The Referral Veterinarian

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Practice type | General practice, 1-3 doctor clinics |
| Pain point | Diagnosing conditions they can't treat, watching clients decline referrals |
| Need | A legitimate option to offer when a client can't afford the specialist |
| Motivation | Retain client relationship, avoid economic euthanasia guilt |

### Tertiary Target: The Proactive Researcher

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Behavior | Researches costs before the diagnosis hits |
| Psychographic | Financially pragmatic, international travel experience |
| Example | "Snowbird" population — comfortable with cross-border movement, owns property or travels regularly |

## 3.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP POSITIONING

Florida is an ideal launch market because:
1. **Geographic proximity** to both Freeport (3-hour ferry) and Cancun (1.5-hour flight).
2. **Large dog population** — ~3.2 million dog-owning households.
3. **High concentration of specialty hospitals** (BluePearl, VCA, UF) generating "sticker shock" moments.
4. **Strong veterinary referral culture** — Florida vets frequently refer to specialists, and face the same affordability conversations daily.
5. **No existing Florida-Bahamas competitor** — the corridor is open for a first mover.
