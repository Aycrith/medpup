### The Markup Chain

U.S. veterinary pharmaceuticals pass through multiple markup layers:
1. **Manufacturer → Distributor:** 10–15% markup
2. **Distributor → Veterinary Clinic:** 15–30% markup
3. **Clinic → Pet Owner:** 100–200% markup

The same medication — often manufactured at the same facility — may cost **2–5x more** through a U.S. veterinary clinic than through a Mexican pharmacy.

## 19.8 REGULATORY CONSIDERATIONS FOR MEDICATION TOURISM

### FDA Personal Importation Policy
The FDA maintains a personal importation policy that generally allows individuals to import a 90-day supply of medications for personal use (including pet medications) under certain conditions:
- The medication is for a serious condition for which effective treatment is not available domestically, OR the domestic product is not affordable
- The product is not considered to pose an unreasonable risk
- The individual seeking to import the product affirms in writing that it is for their own use (or their pet's use)

### U.S. Customs Declaration
Pet medications must be declared at CBP. Having:
- A valid prescription or letter from the U.S. veterinarian
- The original medication packaging with pharmacy labels
- A receipt from the foreign pharmacy
…facilitates smooth customs clearance.

### State Veterinary Practice Act Considerations
Some states restrict veterinarians from dispensing prescriptions without a valid VCPR (Veterinarian-Client-Patient Relationship). MedPup does not dispense — it facilitates the client's access to a licensed Bahamian veterinarian who establishes their own VCPR.

## 19.9 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **The $34.4 billion supplies/medications category growing at 4.4% annually represents an untapped market for MedPup.** No competitor in the cross-border pet medical tourism space has built a dedicated medication tourism product.
2. **Medication tourism creates recurring revenue.** Unlike one-time surgical trips, chronic medication refills create subscription-like repeat trips every 3–6 months — dramatically increasing client lifetime value.
3. **The 70–85% medication price differential mirrors the surgical cost differential.** The same structural factors driving surgical tourism (labor costs, regulatory overhead, pharmaceutical markup chains) apply equally to medications.
4. **FDA personal importation policy provides a legal framework.** As long as medications are for personal/pet use, in reasonable quantities, and properly declared, cross-border medication acquisition is legally defensible.
5. **Medication pickup can be bundled with surgical trips.** A client traveling for dental surgery can also refill chronic medications, increasing trip value without incremental logistics cost.
