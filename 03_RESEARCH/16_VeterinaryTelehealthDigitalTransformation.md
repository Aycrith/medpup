## 16.4 TELEMEDICINE'S ROLE IN CROSS-BORDER CARE

### Pre-Trip Application
- **Virtual pre-consultation:** U.S. vet performs telehealth exam, confirms diagnosis, determines suitability for travel.
- **Document review:** Telemedicine platform used to review health certificates, lab results before departure.
- **Client education:** Virtual session with concierge to walk through trip logistics.

### Post-Trip Application
- **Virtual follow-up:** Mexican/Bahamian surgeon conducts post-op check via telemedicine.
- **U.S. vet handoff:** Surgeon briefs U.S. vet virtually with surgical report and images.
- **Complication triage:** Client sends wound photos; remote vet assesses whether in-person visit needed.

### MedPup Integration Opportunity
MedPup could partner with a telemedicine platform (Dutch, Vetster, AirVet) to offer bundled pre-trip and post-trip virtual consultations. This would:
- Add a revenue stream (telemedicine consult fee)
- Increase client confidence (U.S. vet "involved" virtually)
- Reduce post-op complication risk (early detection via photo review)

## 16.5 AI & DIGITAL DIAGNOSTICS TRENDS

Recent Veterinary Telemedicine Market Trends point to widespread integration of AI and IoT technologies into telemedicine platforms:

- Veterinarians increasingly use AI-powered tools to analyze images, interpret radiographs, and assist in clinical decision-making during virtual consultations.
- Telemedicine service providers are incorporating machine learning algorithms to streamline workflows, support preventive animal care, and provide predictive health insights.
- Mobile penetration and growing preference for contactless care solutions among tech-savvy pet owners are accelerating adoption.[reference:13]

## 16.6 INTERNATIONAL ACCREDITATION & STANDARDS

### AVMA Council on Education (COE)

The AVMA COE is the accrediting body responsible for setting veterinary education standards and assessing veterinary colleges to meet minimum requirements for graduating day-one-ready veterinarians. There are currently 30 accredited veterinary schools in the United States, 5 in Canada, and 18 international programs.

**Key 2025 developments:**
- Proposed revisions to accreditation standards across seven areas: organization, clinical resources, students, admissions, faculty, curriculum, and outcomes (comment period through June 2025)[reference:14]
- COE accreditation decisions are independent of the AVMA and not influenced by partner organizations[reference:15]

### Relevance to MedPup
When vetting partner clinics in Mexico and the Bahamas, MedPup should verify:
- Surgeon credentials against AVMA-recognized or equivalent certification bodies
- Clinic adherence to WHO Surgical Safety Checklist (veterinary adaptation)
- International standards for surgical facilities (equivalent to AAHA accreditation in the U.S.)

## 16.7 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **Telemedicine bridges the access gap.** With 129 million Americans in vet-shortage areas and 7+ day wait times, telemedicine is not a nice-to-have — it's filling a structural gap. MedPup's concierge model can integrate telemedicine for pre-trip consults and post-op follow-ups.
2. **Low current adoption (9%) with high consideration (34%) signals a market about to tip.** Pet owners are aware of telemedicine and increasingly open to it. MedPup can be an early adopter in the cross-border space.
3. **$35 average telemedicine visit vs. $75+ in-person** creates a compelling value proposition for pre-screening and follow-up — reducing the total cost of the MedPup journey even further.
4. **AI diagnostics will change the game.** As AI-powered image analysis becomes standard, remote assessment of surgical outcomes will become more reliable, further reducing the need for in-person follow-ups and making cross-border care even more viable.
