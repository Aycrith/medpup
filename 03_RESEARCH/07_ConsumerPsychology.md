## 7.1 THE FINANCIAL PAIN POINT: Quantified

### Gallup / PetSmart Charities State of Pet Care Study (2025)

Survey of U.S. pet owners and 933 practicing veterinarians, conducted September 9–October 5, 2025.

| Finding | Data |
|---------|------|
| Pet owners who skipped/deferred veterinary care due to cost | **~50%** (nearly half) |
| Pet owners who skipped care who cited cost as reason | **70% (7 in 10)** |
| Veterinarians reporting client finances limit recommended care | **94%** (38% "often," 56% "sometimes") |
| Veterinarians who said cost is cited more than twice as often as any other reason for declined treatment | **>2x** |
| Veterinarians who received NO formal training on discussing financial barriers | **48%** |
| Veterinarians who felt "minimally prepared" for cost conversations | **32%** |
| Veterinarians reporting euthanasia due to financial constraints occurs at least sometimes | **41%** |
| Veterinarians who say financial euthanasia is among hardest aspects of practice | **74%** |

**Sources:** PetSmart Charities-Gallup study surveying 933 U.S. veterinarians found 94% report clients' financial limitations sometimes (56%) or often (38%) prevent delivery of recommended care. Cost was cited more than twice as often as any other reason for declined treatment.[reference:4] In the companion pet parent study, 73% of pet owners who declined care due to cost said they were not offered a lower-priced option.[reference:5]

### The "Alternative Care" Perception Gap

| Stakeholder Perception | Percentage |
|------------------------|------------|
| Veterinarians who say they "often or always" recommend alternative care when cost is a barrier | **81%** |
| Pet owners who declined care and said they were NOT offered a lower-priced option | **73%** |
| Veterinarians who provide financing/payment plans | **41%** |
| Pet owners who recall being offered one | **23%** |

This gap reveals a systemic communication failure: vets think they're offering alternatives; pet owners don't perceive them. MedPup fills this gap by being the clear, concrete alternative.

## 7.3 THE "SURVIVALIST" CLIENT PSYCHOLOGY

Clients considering cross-border pet surgery are not bargain hunters. They are "survivalists" — people who refuse to give up on their pets despite impossible U.S. prices.

### The Gus Case (Portland, OR)

Thomas and Loralee Kitts faced a $12,000 IVDD spinal surgery quote for their dachshund Gus. "We were at a loss. My wife and I were sitting there wondering, what the hell should we do? This is our little guy." They considered euthanasia before finding Vet Voyage. "Loralee Kitts remembered hearing about people going to places like Mexico for less expensive medical care and wondered if such services were available for pets. She hopped online and within minutes, came across the website of Vet Voyage."[reference:10]

### The Coco Case (San Diego)

"After Coco the dog ate a rock in San Diego, its owners were quoted $10,000 to get the care she needed. Across the border, she received an exploratory surgery for roughly $2,000."[reference:11]

### The Henry Case (NYC)

One NYC dog owner faced a $3,500 to $5,000 bill for dental extractions at home and ended up paying roughly a third of that in Mexico. Services like MexiVet Express now transport around 80 pets to Tijuana clinics every week.[reference:12]

## 7.4 THE DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK

When a pet owner receives an unaffordable specialist quote, their decision tree follows this path:

1. **Quote shock:** "I can't afford this."
2. **Guilt spiral:** "What kind of pet parent am I if I can't afford care?"
3. **Research phase:** Google search for "affordable [procedure] for dogs" or "cheap vet surgery near me."
4. **Option evaluation:** Payment plans (rare), CareCredit (debt), crowdfunding (uncertain), cross-border (unfamiliar but compelling).
5. **Trust verification:** "Is it safe? Will my dog be okay? Is this legal?"
6. **Decision:** Either proceed with cross-border option OR decline care/euthanize.

MedPup must intervene at **Step 3 (Research Phase)** with search-optimized content, and at **Step 5 (Trust Verification)** with social proof, clinic photos, credentials, and testimonials.

## 7.6 PSYCHOLOGICAL PRICE POINTS

| U.S. Quote | Likely Client Response |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Under $500 | Most proceed without hesitation |
| $500–$1,500 | Some hesitation; may shop around |
| $1,500–$3,000 | Significant hesitation; actively seeks alternatives |
| $3,000–$5,000 | "Breaking point" for many households; high research activity |
| $5,000+ | Crisis mode; cross-border or euthanasia become active considerations |
| $10,000+ | Most cannot proceed without financing or cross-border option |

The Australian parallel confirms: 4 out of 5 pet owners believe $3,000 is too much to pay for veterinary care (PetSure, 2024).
