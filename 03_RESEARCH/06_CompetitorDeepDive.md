## 6.1 MEXIVET EXPRESS (San Diego → Tijuana)

### Company Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Founded | 2018 by Anna Ginsky |
| Headquarters | San Diego, CA |
| Model | Door-to-door pet courier — picks up pets in SoCal, crosses border to Tijuana, returns same day |
| Founder story | Ginsky was quoted $2,000+ for her own dog's dental work; crossed border and saved $1,600 |
| Scale | Transports approximately **80 pets to Tijuana clinics every week** (as of 2025–2026) |
| Courier fee | Starting at **$125** per trip |
| Team | Multiple drivers/translators making "multiple trips to and from Tijuana each day, with up to five pets (dogs or cats) at a time" |
| Media coverage | ABC Nightline (June 2024), New York Post (March 2026), The Cut/NY Mag, NBC 7 San Diego |

**Sources:** ABC News reports MexiVet Express transports around 80 pets to Tijuana clinics every week. The New York Post documents the door-to-door concierge model: "Companies like MexiVet Express have turned medical tourism for pets into a door-to-door concierge service. For a fee, the company picks up pets in Southern California, navigates the notoriously long border wait times, translates for the Mexican veterinarians, and brings the animals back home the same day."[reference:0]

### Operational Model

- Full-service middleman: lines up appointments, translates, organizes paperwork, helps choose clinics
- Drivers pick up pets, shuttle to Tijuana, stay in contact during appointments, return pets same day
- Savings: 50–90% compared to San Diego prices
- Not a medical provider — stresses it is "just the middleman — working with what it calls a network of experienced, reputable clinics in Tijuana"[reference:1]

### Documented Case Studies

| Case | U.S. Quote | Mexico Cost | Savings |
|------|------------|-------------|---------|
| Henry (dachshund, dental extractions, NYC) | $3,500–$5,000 | ~$600–$1,200 | ~70% |
| Coco (dog ate rock, San Diego) | $10,000 | ~$2,000 | 80% |
| Wes Daniel (multiple procedures) | ~$4,000 | ~$2,000 | 50% |

### Key Differentiator vs. MedPup
**Owner does NOT accompany pet.** MexiVet acts as courier; the pet travels without the owner. This is efficient but lacks the emotional reassurance of the MedPup model where the client and pet stay together throughout the journey.

## 6.2 VET PLAYAS (Tijuana Clinic)

### Company Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Owner | Ronald Richards, DVM |
| Location | Tijuana, Mexico (near San Ysidro border) |
| U.S. client share | **~80% of clients are from the United States** |
| Key services | Wellness exams, dental, orthopedic surgery, diagnostics, emergency care |
| Pricing benchmark | Spay/Neuter: $90–$120 vs. $300+ in San Diego; TTA (TPLO-equivalent) surgery: $1,975 all-inclusive |

**Sources:** Richards told ABC News that "80% of his clients are from the U.S." and explained that labor costs are the primary price difference driver. Vet Playas publishes TTA surgery pricing at $1,975 including anesthesia, medications, and post-operative care.[reference:2]

### Business Model
- Up to 70% lower costs on surgeries and dental work
- Transparent pricing with no surprise fees
- Free consultations and follow-up discounts
- "U.S.-grade quality care at Mexican prices" — clean, modern, U.S.-style facilities
- Bilingual staff accustomed to cross-border clients
- Partners with shuttle services like MexiVet Express for transport

## 6.7 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP POSITIONING

The competitive landscape reveals **five structural gaps** MedPup can exploit:

1. **Geographic gap:** The dominant corridor is California-Tijuana. The Florida-Bahamas/Cancun corridor has no dedicated concierge competitor.
2. **Emotional gap:** Existing courier models separate owner from pet. MedPup's accompanied model builds trust and reduces anxiety — a differentiator for high-anxiety procedures.
3. **Financing gap:** No existing competitor actively promotes CareCredit acceptance at partner clinics.
4. **Veterinary relationship gap:** No competitor has built a formal vet referral network paying post-op follow-up fees.
5. **East Coast density gap:** Florida's 3.2 million dog-owning households are currently unserved by any cross-border pet medical tourism concierge.
