The emotional connection is bidirectional — 58% of pet owners report anxiety when separated from their pet, and 48% believe their pet experiences separation anxiety. This deep emotional bond is the psychological foundation of the humanization economy.[reference:33]

## 22.7 HUMANIZATION SPENDING PATTERNS

The APPA data shows the hierarchy of pet spending, with humanization driving premiumization across every category:

| Spending Category | Humanization Signal |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Food & treats** ($68.3B) | Premium, breed-specific, life-stage, raw, human-grade |
| **Veterinary care** ($41B) | Advanced diagnostics, specialty surgery, cancer treatment |
| **Supplies/medications** ($34.4B) | Supplements, CBD, preventive care products |
| **Other services** ($14.3B) | Grooming, insurance, boarding, training, pet sitting |

The "other services" category grew fastest at 8% YoY, reflecting demand for services that treat pets as family members deserving of professional care.[reference:36]

## 22.11 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **Pet humanization is the macroeconomic tailwind behind everything MedPup does.** When 75.6% of U.S. adults live in dog-owning households and pet spending has grown uninterrupted since 2009, the addressable market is structurally expanding — not contracting.
2. **The premiumization trend in pet food directly parallels the premiumization trend in pet healthcare.** The same owner buying $80 bags of breed-specific raw food is the owner who will pursue TPLO surgery for their dog — and will be shocked by the $5,200 quote. They are already spending significantly on their pet's wellbeing.
3. **Florida is the ideal market.** With record-high pet ownership growth, $9.1 billion in annual pet spending, and geographic proximity to the Bahamas, Florida concentrates all the conditions MedPup needs.
4. **The generational shift favors MedPup.** Millennials (70% pet ownership) and Gen Z are digital-native, research-savvy, and comfortable with non-traditional solutions — including cross-border services. They are also the generations most burdened by student debt and housing costs, making them acutely price-sensitive for large expenditures.
5. **The $483.5 billion projected pet care market in 2035 means MedPup's addressable market will more than double over the next decade** — even without geographic expansion.
