## 11.2 AIRLINE PET POLICIES (Cancun Route)

### Major Carriers on Florida-Mexico Routes

| Airline | In-Cabin Pets | Checked/Cargo Pets | Fee (Each Way) | Restrictions |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **American Airlines** | Yes (carrier under seat) | Yes (cargo) | $125 (cabin), $200+ (cargo) | Max 20 lbs with carrier for cabin |
| **Spirit Airlines** | Yes (carrier under seat) | No cargo | $125 | Max 8" H x 18" L x 14" W carrier |
| **Southwest** | Yes (carrier under seat) | No cargo | $125 | 6 pets per flight |
| **Delta** | Yes (carrier under seat) | Yes (cargo via Delta Cargo) | $125 (cabin), varies (cargo) | No snub-nosed breeds in cargo |
| **United** | Yes (carrier under seat) | Yes (PetSafe cargo program) | $125 (cabin), varies (cargo) | Breed restrictions apply |

**Key operational constraints for MedPup Cancun trips:**
- Large dogs (>20 lbs including carrier) cannot fly in-cabin on any major U.S. carrier
- Cargo transport requires additional health certificates and temperature restrictions (no pets accepted if ground temp >85°F or <20°F)
- Spirit and Southwest do not offer cargo pet transport — American, Delta, or United required for large dogs
- Snub-nosed breeds (French Bulldogs, Pugs, Boston Terriers) face additional restrictions in cargo on some airlines

**Source:** Official airline pet policies, cross-referenced with DOT reporting requirements.

## 11.4 USDA PET TRAVEL DOCUMENTATION INDUSTRY

### Health Certificate Market Size

- USDA APHIS endorsed approximately **1.1 million health certificates** annually (pre-2024 baseline)
- Post-August 2024 CDC rule change, certification requirements increased for dogs from high-risk rabies countries
- Accredited veterinarian count: ~7,000 nationally
- Average cost to pet owner for health certificate: $150-$350 (exam + certificate + endorsement fees)

### Click2Clear (Bahamas e-Permit System)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Platform | Click2Clear (Bahamas government) |
| Cost | $11.20 per pet |
| Processing time | Typically 24-48 hours |
| Required documents | Health certificate, rabies certificate, photo of pet |
| Validity | Single entry; apply for each trip |

## 11.7 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP OPERATIONS

1. **Ferry is the logistics advantage:** Unlike the California-Tijuana corridor with its long vehicle border waits, the Florida-Bahamas ferry is a scheduled, predictable crossing with no congestion delay risk.
2. **Air cargo is the Cancun constraint:** For large dogs on the Cancun route, cargo transport adds complexity (temperature restrictions, breed restrictions, additional cost). This should be factored into Cancun pricing and client communication.
3. **Documentation is a value-add, not a commodity:** The complexity of USDA health certificates, Click2Clear permits, and screwworm certificates creates a natural barrier for DIY cross-border pet travel. MedPup's documentation handling is a genuine value-add that clients cannot easily replicate.
4. **Pet travel kit revenue:** A small ancillary revenue stream exists in selling travel kits; even if modest, it improves the client experience and reinforces the "we handle everything" brand promise.
