## 23.3 THE IN-HOME CARE REVOLUTION

The pet hospice and end-of-life market is increasingly shifting toward distributed, franchise-like in-home care networks, where services are delivered through licensed veterinarians coordinated through centralized platforms.

| Trend | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **In-home euthanasia networks** | Mobile teams expanding into large and mid-sized cities. Providers like Lap of Love expanded telehospice consultations and at-home euthanasia networks across multiple regions. |

| **Transitional care units** | Veterinary hospitals adding dedicated units that guide families through end-of-life and connect them with cremation partners. |

## 23.7 THE ECONOMIC EUTHANASIA CONNECTION

The end-of-life market exists on a spectrum where "economic euthanasia" sits at one extreme:

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| **Treatable condition + affordable care** | Surgery performed, pet recovers |
| **Treatable condition + unaffordable U.S. quote** | Economic euthanasia risk |
| **Treatable condition + MedPup alternative** | Surgery performed abroad, pet recovers |
| **Terminal condition + palliative care** | Hospice, comfort-focused treatment |
| **Terminal condition + suffering** | Humane euthanasia |

MedPup's role is to move pets from the "economic euthanasia risk" category to the "surgery performed, pet recovers" category — preventing unnecessary entry into the end-of-life market.

## 23.8 THE MARKET'S EMOTIONAL FOUNDATION

The end-of-life market's rapid growth (9.57% CAGR) reveals the depth of the human-animal bond:
- Families are choosing formal care for aging pets
- Owners strongly value structured care at the end of life
- The trend shows movement toward quiet, familiar environments that reduce stress for both animal and family
- Growth in these services increases demand for follow-up cremation and funeral options matching the same standard of care

This same emotional bond — the willingness to invest in quality end-of-life care — is what drives owners to seek life-extending surgical options like those MedPup facilitates.

## 23.9 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **The end-of-life market validates the willingness to pay.** When owners spend $300–$2,000 on euthanasia and cremation, they are demonstrating profound investment in their pet's entire lifecycle. The willingness to spend $1,500–$3,800 on life-saving surgery abroad is consistent with this psychology.
2. **Economic euthanasia is the competitor MedPup exists to defeat.** Every pet euthanized for a treatable condition is a market failure MedPup can address. The 66% economic euthanasia rate represents not just a tragedy but a massive addressable market.
3. **The full spectrum model suggests service line extensions.** MedPup could eventually offer:
   - **Palliative care tourism:** Affordable pain management and hospice medications from abroad
   - **Diagnostic tourism:** Advanced imaging to determine treatability before committing to surgery
   - **Medication tourism:** Chronic disease management at international prices
4. **The 9.57% CAGR in end-of-life services signals the pet care market is maturing.** As it matures, consumers will demand comprehensive, affordable solutions across the entire care spectrum — a demand MedPup is positioned to meet.
5. **Partnerships with hospice providers could create a referral pipeline.** When a pet is diagnosed with a terminal but treatable condition (e.g., operable cancer), the hospice provider could refer to MedPup for surgical intervention before the case progresses to palliative-only status.
