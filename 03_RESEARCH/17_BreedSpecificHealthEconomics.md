## 17.1 THE FRENCH BULLDOG: MEDPUP'S HIGHEST-VALUE TARGET BREED

The French Bulldog has become the most popular purebred dog in America and simultaneously the most expensive to keep healthy. This breed alone represents a concentrated addressable market for MedPup.

### French Bulldog Health Condition Costs (U.S. Specialty)

| Condition | Prevalence | U.S. Surgical Cost | Description |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| **IVDD (Intervertebral Disc Disease)** | Common (chondrodystrophic anatomy) | **$9,000-$12,000** (imaging + routine decompression) | One of the most common neurologic conditions in the breed. Hansen type 1 disc extrusions typically occur in dogs 2+ years old. |
| **Hemivertebrae** | **>80%** of French Bulldogs | $3,500-$4,500 (imaging alone); $9,000-$11,500 (with decompression) | Often incidental, but some require advanced stabilization. |
| **BOAS (Brachycephalic Obstructive Airway Syndrome)** | Very common | $3,000-$5,000 | Airway surgery for flat-faced breeds. |
| **Meningoencephalitis / Brain Tumors** | Elevated risk | $3,500-$4,500 (imaging alone) | French Bulldogs prone to gliomas presenting in younger populations with poorer prognosis. |
| **Humeral Fractures** | Elevated risk | Variable | Surgical concern specific to the breed. |
| **Skin Allergies** | Very common | Ongoing management cost | Frequent vet visits, medications, specialized diets. |

**Sources:** Metropolitan Veterinary Associates Winter 2025 Newsletter reports average cost for IVDD imaging and routine decompression at $9,000-$12,000, with cost depending on the pet's neurologic status at time of diagnosis. Over 80% of French Bulldogs suffer from hemivertebrae. Hemivertebrae imaging alone averages $3,500-$4,500, and with decompression $9,000-$11,500. BOAS and humeral fractures are additional surgical concerns.[reference:16][reference:17]

### Why French Bulldogs Matter to MedPup

- A single French Bulldog owner facing an IVDD diagnosis ($9,000-$12,000 U.S. quote) is likely to cross the psychological breaking point for affordability.
- With >80% of the breed having hemivertebrae, the addressable pool is large and recurring.
- French Bulldog owners tend to be deeply attached ("Velcro dogs"), making them highly motivated to seek alternatives to euthanasia.
- The breed's popularity means concentrated demand in urban areas (Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Tampa).

## 17.4 MIXED-BREED DOGS: THE MAJORITY MARKET

Mixed-breed dogs generally have fewer claims and lower insurance premiums than purebreds. Their genetic diversity provides some protection against breed-specific hereditary conditions. However, they are still susceptible to:
- CCL tears (any medium-large dog)
- Dental disease (all dogs)
- Mass/tumor development (age-related, all dogs)
- Fractures (trauma, all dogs)

**MedPup implication:** While breed-specific marketing targets the highest-concentration surgical candidates, broad marketing (cost savings, general dental, general orthopedics) captures the mixed-breed majority.

## 17.5 THE COST SURGE: WHY BREED HEALTH ECONOMICS MATTER NOW

Veterinary costs have climbed **38.5% since 2019**, with emergency visits now exceeding $600 on average. Nearly half of pet owners say emergency costs have been higher than expected, and many report delaying or avoiding treatment because of financial concerns.[reference:19]

Combined with breed predispositions, the math is devastating:
- French Bulldog with IVDD: $9,000-$12,000 at a time when costs are up 38.5%
- Labrador with bilateral CCL tears: $9,000-$12,000 for two TPLOs
- Golden Retriever with cancer: $5,000-$15,000 for surgery and chemotherapy

These are the "economic euthanasia" scenarios that MedPup directly addresses.

---

## 17.6 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **French Bulldogs and Dachshunds are the "power users" of cross-border veterinary care.** Their high prevalence of IVDD and BOAS, combined with extreme U.S. surgical costs, make them the ideal early adopter breed segments.
2. **Breed-specific Facebook groups are the highest-converting marketing channels.** A single positive case study shared in a French Bulldog IVDD support group can generate dozens of inquiries.
3. **Mixed-breed dogs are the volume market.** While breed-specific marketing captures high-value surgical cases, the mixed-breed majority provides volume for dental, mass removal, and spay/neuter day-trips.
4. **The 38.5% cost increase since 2019 is not reversing.** Structural factors (PE consolidation, workforce shortage, pharmaceutical markups) will continue pushing U.S. prices higher, expanding MedPup's addressable market year over year.
