## 18.1 THE LIFETIME COST DISCONNECT

The 2025 Synchrony Pet Lifetime of Care Study reveals a profound gap between what pet owners expect to pay and what they actually pay.

| Pet Type | Actual Lifetime Cost Range | Owner Estimate | Gap |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------|-----|
| **Dog** (15-year lifespan) | **$22,125 – $60,602** | ~$8,200 | Owners underestimate by **63-86%** |
| **Cat** (lifespan) | **$20,073 – $47,106** | <$6,000 | Owners underestimate by **70-87%** |
| **Pocket pet** (6-year lifespan) | **$7,600 – $14,938** | ~$3,000 | Owners underestimate by **60-80%** |

**Key finding:** Nearly **8 out of 10 pet owners underestimate lifetime pet care costs.** Only 31% of pet owners say they can comfortably manage a major pet expense, while 74% have faced unexpected pet care costs exceeding $250.[reference:20]

**Sources:** Synchrony's 2025 Pet Lifetime of Care Study, reported by Vetsource Veterinary Analytics. Since the 2022 study, the cost to care for a dog for 15 years increased by 11.65%. Cat care costs rose 19.4%. For the first time, pocket pets were included.[reference:21]

## 18.2 CARE AVOIDANCE BEHAVIOR

### Covetrus/Stripe 2025 Pet Owner Cost & Care Survey

Survey of more than 1,000 U.S. pet owners:

| Behavior | All Pet Owners | Those Who Previously Struggled to Pay |
|----------|---------------|--------------------------------------|
| Delayed treatment | **24%** | **41%** |
| Opted out of a procedure | **21%** | **31%** |
| Skipped a visit entirely | **20%** | **32%** |

**Sources:** Covetrus and Stripe partnered to survey more than 1,000 pet owners. Among all pet owners: 24% delayed treatment, 21% opted out of a procedure, 20% skipped a visit altogether. For those who previously struggled to pay: 41% delayed treatment, 31% opted out, 32% skipped care entirely.[reference:22]

### The Vicious Cycle

"These behaviors don't just put pets at risk — they weaken client loyalty and cut into practice revenue. Every delayed diagnostic, postponed dental, or skipped surgery is both a lost health outcome and a lost client touchpoint that fuels a vicious cycle: conditions worsen, costs climb higher, and clients become more likely to avoid care again in the future."[reference:23]

### MetLife Pet Insurance Survey (2025)

| Finding | Percentage |
|---------|------------|
| Delayed or skipped a vet visit due to cost concerns | **70%** (nearly 3 in 4) |
| Cost of veterinary care affected peace of mind | **85%** (>4 in 5) |
| Worry at least occasionally about affording future vet visits | **75%** |

## 18.3 THE BILL SHOCK PHENOMENON

### Emergency Cost Reality

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average scheduled visit cost | **$278** (median $175) |
| Average emergency visit cost | **$618** (median $350) |

## 18.4 WHAT PET OWNERS VALUE MOST

When asked how important different factors are in choosing or staying with a veterinarian:

| Factor | Importance |
|--------|-----------|
| **Pricing transparency** | **86%** said important |
| Ability to use preferred payment method | **79%** |
| Having the lowest price in the area | **54%** |
| Ability to spread out payments | **49%** |

**Key insight:** Pricing transparency ranks higher than actual low prices. Pet owners want to know what they'll pay before they commit — exactly the value proposition MedPup offers with clear concierge fees and clinic price lists.[reference:26]

## 18.7 PAYMENT BEHAVIOR & FINANCING PREFERENCES

### CareCredit / Synchrony Findings (2025 Study)

- Dog owners are the **least likely** among pet groups to seek out financing options or payment plans, despite being the most likely to face large unexpected bills.[reference:28]
- 25% of small companion animal owners use health and pet care credit cards, and they are 36% more likely than other pet owners to borrow money from friends or family.[reference:29]
- Nearly 1 in 10 cat owners adjusted other expenses, such as housing or car payments, to pay care costs.[reference:30]

### MedPup Implication
Many dog owners resist financing (CareCredit) because they see it as debt, not a solution. MedPup's value proposition — "lower the principal so you don't need as much financing" — addresses this psychological resistance directly.
