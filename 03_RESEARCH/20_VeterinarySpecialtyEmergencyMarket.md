The market has shown strong growth, driven by increasing pet ownership, greater veterinary healthcare awareness, rising demand for pet insurance, growing emphasis on preventive care, and an increasing number of veterinary clinics.[reference:9]

### Imaging Modalities in Cross-Border Context
Advanced imaging (CT, MRI) is often priced at a premium in the U.S.:
- **CT scan:** $1,000–$3,000 (U.S.) vs. $300–$800 (Mexico)
- **MRI:** $2,500–$5,000 (U.S.) vs. $600–$1,500 (Mexico)

This creates a parallel imaging tourism opportunity — clients could obtain advanced diagnostics abroad at 60–80% savings even before committing to surgery.

| Projected (2034) | $1.19–$1.29 billion | Multiple analysts |

Cancer is a leading cause of death in older dogs, with approximately 1 in 4 dogs developing neoplasia in their lifetime. The pet cancer therapeutics market is growing rapidly as:
- Pet lifespans increase (better nutrition, preventive care)
- Owners pursue advanced treatment options previously reserved for humans
- Chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and targeted therapies become more available

For MedPup, oncology represents a high-value surgical and medical tourism opportunity. Tumor removal surgeries at U.S. specialty hospitals cost $3,000–$15,000, while the same procedures at partner clinics in Mexico or the Bahamas cost 60–80% less.[reference:10]

## 20.6 THE STAFFING CRISIS IN SPECIALTY & EMERGENCY CARE

### Instinct Science 2025 Emergency & Urgent Care Survey

| Finding | Percentage |
|---------|------------|
| Practices citing staffing shortages as major issue | **85%** (up from 78% in 2024) |
| Practices that hired more full-time staff in 2025 | **55%** |
| Staff reporting working MORE hours despite hiring | **32%** |

**Key insight:** Hiring is lagging behind workloads. Even when practices add staff, workload growth outpaces hiring — meaning wait times continue to increase and service availability continues to decline.[reference:11]

### Veterinarian Burnout

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Veterinarians reporting emotional exhaustion or burnout | **Up to 50%** |
| Most acute in | Early-career vets under 35 |
| AVMA estimated U.S. veterinarians (2025) | **133,475** |
| Working in clinical practice | **>70%** |
| Burnout rate (broader workforce) | **40%** |

The shortage extends beyond veterinarians to veterinary technicians. Reports indicate an ongoing shortage of both vets and vet techs, causing long wait times for pet owners and increased stress levels among clinic staff. The shortage does not appear to be decreasing anytime soon.[reference:12][reference:13]

## 20.7 CORPORATE CONSOLIDATION IN SPECIALTY CARE

### The 2025 AVMA Economic Report Findings

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Corporate groups' ownership of U.S. practices | **~30%** |
| Share of companion-animal revenue generated by corporate groups | **>50%** |
| Trend in 2025 | Shift from rapid acquisition to financial discipline |

**Key finding:** Corporate-owned practices generate more than half of companion-animal revenue despite owning only ~30% of practices — indicating they are disproportionately concentrated in higher-revenue specialty and multi-doctor practices.[reference:14]

### Major M&A Activity
- **$8.6 billion** combination of Southern Veterinary Partners and Mission Veterinary Partners
- After years of rapid consolidation, investors and corporate groups are now prioritizing operational efficiency over expansion
- Early movers like Thrive Pet Healthcare secured fresh financing to stabilize operations and rebuild margins, but not every growth story succeeded
- Regulatory scrutiny of veterinary consolidation is increasing[reference:15][reference:16]

### MedVet Expansion
By November 2025, MedVet expanded by launching new multi-specialty and emergency hospitals in Atlanta, Austin, and Phoenix. These facilities emphasize a veterinarian-led ownership structure — an alternative to increasing corporate consolidation.[reference:17]

## 20.9 KEY INSIGHT FOR MEDPUP

1. **The $22.1 billion emergency care market creates a constant stream of "sticker shock" moments.** When a dog presents at a BluePearl emergency room with GDV (bloat) and the owner faces a $5,000–$8,000 estimate, they are primed for an alternative — even if not for that specific emergency, the experience makes them receptive to MedPup for future non-emergency surgeries.
2. **The 85% staffing shortage figure is structural, not cyclical.** As emergency and specialty practices struggle to staff, wait times increase, access decreases, and prices rise — all expanding MedPup's addressable market.
3. **Corporate consolidation concentrates high prices.** With >50% of companion-animal revenue flowing through corporate-owned practices that charge 10–30% more on average, the premium-priced market segment is growing faster than the independent segment.
4. **Oncology is the next frontier.** At 9.8–11.7% CAGR, pet cancer therapeutics is one of the fastest-growing segments. MedPup should build oncology-specific trip offerings (tumor removal surgery, chemotherapy administration) as the Cancun route matures.
5. **Imaging tourism could be a standalone product.** With CT/MRI scans costing 60–80% less abroad, a "Diagnostic Day-Trip" for advanced imaging could generate leads that convert to surgical trips.
