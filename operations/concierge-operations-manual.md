## 1. Overview & Role

### What MedPup Is

MedPup is a **veterinary logistics concierge, NOT a veterinary practice.** We organize safe, stress-free trips from Florida to pre-vetted international clinics. We handle all logistics — transportation, permits, documentation, clinic scheduling, on-site support.

### What the Concierge Does

The concierge is the client's single point of contact from the moment they book through post-trip follow-up. Responsibilities include:

- **Pre-trip:** Document collection, permit submission, scheduling, client communication
- **Trip day:** Meet-and-greet at departure point, document management, customs clearance, clinic liaison, on-site support
- **Post-trip:** Follow-up email, surgical report distribution, feedback collection, Go-Bag restock

### Critical Rules

1. **Never make medical recommendations.** Refer all medical questions to the veterinarian.
2. **Never handle controlled substances.** All medications are dispensed by the clinic.
3. **Never guarantee outcomes.** MedPup coordinates logistics; the clinic provides medical care.
4. **Always carry originals.** Client documents stay with the concierge until U.S. Customs clearance.
5. **Always stay with the pets.** The concierge remains at the clinic during all surgeries.

## 2. Trip Archetypes

### Freeport Day-Trip ("The Pharmacy Ferry")

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Travel** | Balearia Caribbean ferry, Fort Lauderdale ↔ Freeport |
| **Duration** | 6:00 AM – 8:30 PM (same day) |

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Travel** | Group charter flight, Miami ↔ Cancun |
| **Duration** | Thursday – Sunday (4 days, 3 nights) |
| **Procedures** | TPLO, IVDD, oncology, BOAS, advanced diagnostics (MRI/CT) |

| **Payment** | Credit card or bank transfer to clinic |
| **Concierge fee** | $1,500/pet |
| **Target margin** | 21% |

## 3. Pre-Trip Preparation (48 Hours Before)

### Check: Trip Readiness

- [ ] Confirm ferry booking for all clients + concierge (Balearia reference #: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_)
- [ ] Verify all pet permits (Click2Clear ePermits) are approved and printed
- [ ] Confirm surgical time slots with partner clinic
- [ ] Send final client list to clinic: pet name, procedure, weight, special needs
- [ ] Verify automated 3-day reminder email was sent to all clients
- [ ] Charge MedPup phone to 100% + pack backup battery

### Check: Client Documents

For each client, confirm they have:

- [ ] USDA-accredited health certificate (original, within 10 days of travel)
- [ ] Rabies vaccination certificate (current, at least 30 days old)
- [ ] Signed Client Service Agreement (digital copy accessible)

### Check: Weather & Contingency

- [ ] Check weather forecast for Freeport / Cancun

- [ ] Print ferry/flight tickets (1 set)
- [ ] Print Click2Clear ePermits (1 set per pet, in waterproof sleeve)
- [ ] Print emergency contact sheet
- [ ] Confirm $100 emergency cash (small bills) in bag

## 4. Trip Day Timeline: Freeport

### 5:45 AM — Arrival at Port Everglades Terminal 21

- [ ] Arrive. Put on MedPup polo (bright blue).
- [ ] Text all clients: *"Good morning! I'm here at Terminal 21 in a blue MedPup polo near the check-in desk. No rush — we board at 6:30."*
- [ ] Locate the check-in counter. Confirm group booking.

### 6:00 AM – 6:20 AM — Client Check-In

For each client, perform Document Check:
- [ ] Verify USDA health certificate is original and signed within 10 days
- [ ] Verify rabies certificate
- [ ] Check pet's ID tag matches documents
- [ ] Collect health certificate (you carry originals until U.S. Customs on return)
- [ ] Give client a MedPup wristband or lanyard

### 6:20 AM — Headcount

- [ ] All clients present? Count pets and humans (including you)
- [ ] Document total on Trip Log

### 6:25 AM — Boarding

- [ ] Board ferry as a group. Assist with carriers. Find seats together.

### 7:00 AM — Ferry Departure

### During Crossing (7:00 AM – 10:00 AM)

- [ ] Second document check. Organize all certificates by pet in waterproof sleeve.
- [ ] Brief clients on day's timeline: arrival, customs, clinic, lunch, return
- [ ] Answer last-minute questions. Reassure nervous clients.
- [ ] Remind: *"You'll pay the clinic directly after the procedure. CareCredit or card."*
- [ ] Distribute seasickness meds if needed
- [ ] Walk dogs in outdoor pet area before arrival

### 10:00 AM — Arrive Freeport Harbour

- [ ] Disembark as a group. Keep pets leashed/carried.
- [ ] Proceed to Bahamas Customs.
- [ ] Present per pet: Click2Clear ePermit, USDA health certificate, rabies certificate
- [ ] Pay any outstanding fees (should be prepaid; carry $20 cash just in case)
- [ ] Once cleared, gather outside customs.

### 10:30 AM — Transit to Clinic

- [ ] Confirm transport (pre-arranged taxi/van or walking)
- [ ] Load pets safely

### 11:00 AM — Clinic Check-In

For each pet:
- [ ] Hand clinic intake form
- [ ] Confirm procedure, weight, fasting status, allergies
- [ ] Have client sign clinic's surgical consent form
- [ ] Confirm payment method (CareCredit/card/cash)
- [ ] Confirm post-op pickup time
- [ ] Clients may explore Freeport during surgery. Set meeting time (e.g., 3:00 PM).
- [ ] **Concierge stays at clinic** for all surgeries.

### During Surgery (11:30 AM – 3:00 PM)

- [ ] Remain in waiting area. Be reachable by phone.
- [ ] Monitor progress. Ask for updates every 60-90 minutes.
- [ ] If complication arises: contact client immediately.
- [ ] Photograph clinic exterior/interior (with permission, no patients).
- [ ] Collect as each pet finishes:
  - [ ] Written post-op instructions
  - [ ] Discharge medications (labeled, with dosing)
  - [ ] E-collar
  - [ ] Surgeon's contact card
  - [ ] Receipt/invoice

### 3:00 PM — Post-Op Pickup

- [ ] Clients return to clinic
- [ ] Review post-op instructions together:
  - [ ] Medication schedule
  - [ ] Activity restriction
  - [ ] Incision care
  - [ ] Signs of complications
  - [ ] Follow-up appointment within 14 days
- [ ] Give each client the Client Packet
- [ ] Take group photo (with permission) for social media / testimonials
- [ ] Depart for harbour. Allow extra time for groggy dogs.

### 3:30 PM – 4:30 PM — Return to Harbour

### 5:00 PM — Return Ferry Departs

- [ ] Board. Help clients settle with recovering dogs.
- [ ] Check each dog: comfortable, e-collars on, not licking incisions.

### During Return Crossing (5:00 PM – 8:00 PM)

- [ ] Prepare for U.S. Customs
- [ ] Organize: USDA health certificates, rabies certificates

### 8:00 PM — Arrive Port Everglades, U.S. Customs

- [ ] Have all documents ready for CBP officer
- [ ] CBP may visually inspect each dog
- [ ] Once cleared, disembark

### 8:30 PM — Client Departure

- [ ] Say goodbye. Remind: *"You'll get a post-trip email tonight with our review link. Call the 24-hour line if anything concerns you."*

- [ ] AM: Post-op check at clinic

- [ ] Final check-up at clinic
- [ ] Screwworm certificate issuance (if applicable)
- [ ] Transport to Cancun airport
- [ ] Group charter return to Miami
- [ ] U.S. Customs clearance
- [ ] Clients depart

## 6. Client Communication Scripts

### Booking Confirmation Call Script

> "Hi [Name], this is [Concierge Name] from MedPup. Your trip for [Pet Name] on [Date] is confirmed! I'll be your concierge for the trip.
>
> Here's what to expect: We'll meet at [Terminal/Airport] at [Time]. The [ferry/flight] takes about [duration]. Your pet's surgery is scheduled at [clinic name] and I'll be with you and [Pet Name] the entire day.
>
> The most important thing between now and then is getting your USDA health certificate. You need to schedule that with your regular vet within 10 days of travel. I'll send a reminder email with full details.
>
> Do you have any questions right now?"

### Late Client Script (Trip Morning)

> *6:15 AM — Call:* "Hi [Name], this is [Concierge]. We're about to board. Where are you right now?"

> *6:25 AM — Text if no answer:* "We must board now. The ferry will not wait. Please call me ASAP at [phone]."

### Complication Script (During Surgery)

> *Call client:* "Hi [Name], this is [Concierge] at the clinic. I wanted to let you know that the surgeon is attending to [Pet Name] — everything is under control. [Brief, calm update]. I'll call again when there's more news. The surgeon will speak with you directly after the procedure."

### Post-Op Review Script

> "Here's what to expect for [Pet Name]'s recovery. [Review medication schedule, activity restriction, incision care, signs of complications, follow-up appointment.] Does that all make sense? Do you have any questions?"

### Post-Trip Check-In (3 Days Later)

> "Hi [Name], this is [Concierge] from MedPup. Just checking in — how is [Pet Name] doing? Any concerns about the incision, appetite, or medications?"

## 7. Emergency Protocols

### Decision Tree

```
INCIDENT OCCURS (medical, missed ferry, lost document, etc.)
                │
                ▼
Is the pet in immediate medical danger?
   ┌──── YES ────► Contact clinic / nearest vet IMMEDIATELY.
   │               Then call client if not present.
   │               Then call MedPup 24-hr line.
   │               Document everything.
   │
   └──── NO ─────► Can you resolve it yourself in 5 minutes?
                      ┌─ YES → Resolve. Log it.
                      └─ NO  → Call MedPup 24-hr line for decision support.
                                Then communicate with client.
```

### Medical Emergency — Concierge Actions

1. **Stay calm.** Your composure sets the tone for everyone.
2. **Alert the clinic vet team immediately.** Do not attempt to diagnose or treat.
3. **Call the client** if they are not present. Use the Complication Script (Section 6).
4. **Call MedPup 24-hour line** for coordination support.
5. **Document everything** — times, actions taken, communications.
6. **Follow up** with client within 2 hours of resolution.

### Missed Ferry/ Flight — Concierge Actions

1. **Attempt contact** immediately at the boarding time.
2. **If no response by final boarding:** client forfeits the trip.
3. **Provide 24-hour line** for rescheduling (additional fee applies).
4. **Notify clinic** of cancellation immediately.
5. **Document in Trip Log.**

### Lost Document — Concierge Actions

1. **Check Go-Bag** — carry digital copies of all permits and agreements.
2. **Call MedPup 24-hour line** — we may have copies on file.
3. **For lost health certificate:** Contact the issuing vet to verify. U.S. Customs may accept a digital copy with verification.
4. **Document the loss** and what was done to resolve it.

### Weather / Ferry Cancellation

1. **Know Balearia's cancellation policy** before the trip.
2. **Contact all clients immediately** if cancellation is likely.
3. **Reschedule** — have next available date ready.
4. **Document in Trip Log.**

## 11. Reference: Import Permit Process

### Freeport (Click2Clear ePermit)

1. Go to click2clear.com
2. Create account (if first time) or log in
3. Select: Apply for Import Permit → Pets
4. Enter: Owner name, pet name, breed, age, microchip #
5. Upload: Rabies certificate page
6. Pay fee: $10.00 + 12% VAT = $11.20
7. Download approved permit (PDF)
8. Print 2 copies — one for customs, one for Go-Bag

**Processing time:** Same day (100% approval guarantee for routine applications)

### Cancun (USDA Endorsement + Screwworm)

1. Client obtains health certificate from USDA-accredited vet
2. Submit to USDA APHIS Veterinary Services office for endorsement
3. Allow 7+ business days for processing
4. Schedule screwworm inspection within Cancun: must be within 5 days of return flight
5. Screwworm certificate signed by Mexican licensed veterinarian
6. Present both health certificate and screwworm cert at U.S. Customs on return
