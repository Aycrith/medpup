## 2. Clinic Qualification Criteria

### Required (Must-Have)

| Criterion | How to Verify |
|-----------|---------------|
| Surgical suite with general anesthesia capability | In-person inspection / video tour |
| Digital X-ray (preferably orthopedic-capable) | Equipment list + photos |
| Licensed veterinarians on staff | Verify via SEP (Secretaría de Educación Pública) database |
| COFEPRIS registration (Mexican FDA equivalent) | Request registration number, verify online |
| English-fluent medical staff | Phone/video call with surgical team |
| Willingness to sign MOU with MedPup | Direct conversation with clinic director |
| Ability to issue itemized receipts (USD) | Sample invoice request |
| Clean facility with proper sterilization protocols | Inspection checklist |

### Strongly Preferred

| Criterion | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Board-certified surgeon (orthopedics) | Critical for TPLO, IVDD — drives client trust |
| In-house lab (bloodwork, urinalysis) | Reduces need for external lab coordination |
| CT or MRI access (on-site or nearby) | Required for IVDD, oncology staging |
| Experience with international/U.S. clients | Familiarity with documentation, payment, communication |
| Acceptance of credit card payments | Avoids cash-only complications |
| Ability to provide written surgical reports in English | Required for U.S. vet follow-up |

### Ideal (Differentiator)

| Criterion | Advantage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Dedicated recovery ward or hospital ward | Better post-op monitoring vs. send-home model |
| WHO Surgical Safety Checklist in use | Measurable quality standard |
| Published price list (transparent) | Enables accurate savings calculators |
| Willingness to offer volume-based pricing | Improves Cancun trip unit economics |
| Referral relationships with local recovery villas | Streamlines accommodation logistics |

## 5. Cancun-Specific MOU Addendum

In addition to the standard Partner Clinic MOU (see `templates/partner-clinic-mou.md`), Cancun agreements should include:

### Screwworm Compliance Clause
> The Clinic agrees to provide or arrange for the issuance of a screwworm inspection certificate (Form VS 1-27) within 5 days of each patient's scheduled return flight to the United States. The certificate must be signed by a full-time salaried veterinary official of Mexico and state that the dog was inspected for screwworm within 5 days preceding shipment.

### USDA Health Certificate Requirement
> The Clinic agrees to review and, if appropriate, sign the USDA APHIS health certificate for each MedPup-referred patient. MedPup will coordinate with the patient's U.S. veterinarian to prepare the certificate; the Clinic's role is to confirm the patient's health status upon arrival.

### Post-Op Report Requirements
> The Clinic agrees to provide a written surgical report for each patient within 24 hours of procedure completion. Reports must include: procedure performed, surgeon name and credentials, findings, medications administered (with dosage and frequency), discharge instructions, and follow-up recommendations. Reports must be provided in English.

## 6. Cancun Operations Addendum

### Regulatory Checklist (Per Trip)

- [ ] USDA-endorsed health certificates obtained (allow 7+ business days)
- [ ] Screwworm inspection scheduled within 5 days of return flight
- [ ] Group charter flight confirmed (MIA→CUN)
- [ ] Recovery villa booked (3 nights)
- [ ] Ground transport arranged (airport ↔ villa ↔ clinic)

### Screwworm Certificate Process

1. **Before trip:** Confirm which Mexican veterinary official can sign the screwworm certificate
2. **During trip (Day 2-3):** Schedule the inspection at the partner clinic
3. **Before return:** Obtain signed original certificate
4. **At U.S. Customs:** Present certificate along with health certificate and rabies cert

### Recovery Villa Requirements

- Pet-friendly (allows dogs in rooms)
- Climate-controlled (Cancun heat — critical for brachycephalic breeds)
- Ground floor or elevator access (no stairs for post-op patients)
- Cleaning supplies available (for accidents, wound care)
- Quiet area (away from bars, nightlife)
- Refrigerator (for medication storage)

### Group Charter Logistics

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Preferred carrier | American Airlines (MIA→CUN, pet-friendly, 1.5 hr) |
| Cabin | Climate-controlled cabin (no cargo for brachycephalic breeds) |
| Group size | 3-5 pets + 2 concierges |
| Booking window | 3-4 weeks in advance |
| Cost target | ~$3,500 per group (charter) or empty-leg booking |

When negotiating with Cancun partner clinics, target these rates:

|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------------------|
| TPLO | $1,200 - $1,500 | $1,000 - $1,200 |

| Dental with Extractions | $150 - $250 | $120 - $200 |
| Mass Removal (Complex) | $250 - $500 | $200 - $400 |
| BOAS Correction | $800 - $1,200 | $700 - $1,000 |
| Oncology Mass Removal | $1,500 - $3,000 | $1,200 - $2,500 |
| MRI | $300 - $500 | $250 - $400 |
| CT Scan | $400 - $800 | $350 - $700 |

**Negotiation leverage:**
- Steady, pre-booked referral stream (not walk-in clients)
- No marketing cost to the clinic
- All documentation managed by MedPup
- Pre-qualified, paying clients (no no-shows)
- Group bookings fill surgical slots that might otherwise be empty

**Fallback position:** If a clinic won't discount, ask for:
- Priority scheduling (first surgery slot of the day)
- No deposit requirements for MedPup patients
- Post-op report included in surgical fee
- Free consultation for MedPup-referred patients
