## 1. Post-Op Care Instructions (Generic)

### IMMEDIATE CARE (First 24 Hours)

Your dog has undergone general anesthesia and surgery:

- Keep in a warm, quiet, confined area (small room or crate)
- Offer small amount of water. If no vomiting after 2 hours, offer small meal (half normal portion)
- Expect grogginess, whining, or disorientation — normal after anesthesia
- **NOT normal:** Pale gums, difficulty breathing, continuous bleeding, inability to stand, severe vomiting — call Clinic immediately

### INCISION CARE

- **Do NOT** get incision wet for 10–14 days. No bathing, no swimming
- **Do NOT** allow licking or chewing. Use e-collar at all times (except supervised feeding)
- Check incision daily. Slight redness/swelling is normal
- **Call your vet if:** discharge (yellow/green), wound opening, foul odor, excessive swelling

### ACTIVITY RESTRICTION

- Leash walks only for bathroom breaks for 7–10 days
- No running, jumping, stair climbing, or rough play
- Confine when unsupervised

## 2. Procedure-Specific Addendums

### Dental Surgery Addendum

- **Diet:** Feed only soft food (canned/pate or soaked kibble) for 5–7 days
- **Pain:** Some drool tinged with blood is normal for 24–48 hours
- **Chewing:** No hard chews, toys, or bones for 14 days
- **Sutures:** Absorbable sutures dissolve in 2–4 weeks. Do not pull
- **Bad Breath:** Temporary odor is normal. Worsening after Day 3 — call vet

### Mass Removal Addendum

- **Bandage:** Keep clean and dry. Change per Clinic instructions
- **Activity:** Strict rest for 10–14 days
- **Suture Removal:** Non-absorbable sutures/staples removed by your vet in 10–14 days
- **Biopsy:** If sent for histopathology, results in 7–10 days. Report sent to you and your vet

### Spay/Neuter Addendum

- **E-collar:** Must be worn at all times for 10–14 days
- **Activity:** Leash walks only. No dog parks or daycare for 14 days
- **Males:** Scrotal swelling common for 3–5 days. Call if increases after Day 5 or becomes painful
- **Females:** Small amount of serous (clear/pink) discharge normal. Call for heavy bleeding or pus

### TPLO/Orthopedic Addendum

- **Weight-bearing:** Some dogs bear weight immediately, others take 3–5 days. Both normal
- **Ice pack:** Apply to surgical site for 15 min, 3–4x daily for first 48 hours
- **Strict confinement:** Crate rest when unsupervised for 8 weeks
- **Rehabilitation:** Passive range-of-motion exercises starting Day 3 (surgeon will demonstrate)
- **Follow-up X-rays:** At 8 weeks post-op to confirm bone healing
