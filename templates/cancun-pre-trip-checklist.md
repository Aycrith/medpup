# Cancun Pre-Trip Checklist — MedPup Surgical Concierge

> Plan ahead so your pet's medical trip goes smoothly. Below is a week-by-week
> timeline covering Mexico import requirements, CDC travel rules, and re-entry.

---

## 4 Weeks Before

- [ ] **Schedule health certificate appointment** with USDA-accredited US veterinarian
      (required for Mexico entry; valid for 10 days after issue — time it near departure)
- [ ] **Book round-trip airline tickets** with pet-in-cabin or cargo reservation
      - Preferred: FLL–CUN direct (FLL = Fort Lauderdale)
      - Confirm pet carrier dimensions meet airline specs
      - Notify airline of service/support animal if applicable
- [ ] **Obtain APHIS endorsement** of the health certificate (USDA APHIS VS Form 7001
      or equivalent; Mexico requires it)
- [ ] **Contact VetCancun** — schedule procedure date & confirm pricing
- [ ] **Verify passport validity** for human travellers (no visa required for US citizens,
      but passport must be valid for entire stay)

---

## 2 Weeks Before

- [ ] **Confirm VetCancun appointment** (procedure date, pre-op instructions, fasting
      requirements)
- [ ] **Book hotel** — pet-friendly, near VetCancun clinic
      (budget $50–$150/night; request first-floor or easy access for post-op pet)
- [ ] **Arrange private transfer** (airport ↔ hotel ↔ vet clinic)
- [ ] **Check airline pet reservation** — re-confirm with airline by phone
- [ ] **Check Mexico travel advisory** on State Department website

---

## 1 Week Before

- [ ] **Gather medical records** — bring to Cancun:
      - Vaccination records (rabies + core vaccines)
      - Microchip documentation
      - Previous surgical history / imaging (X-rays, MRI)
      - Medication list & dosages
- [ ] **Fill prescriptions** — pre-op & post-op medications:
      - Antibiotics, pain relief, sedatives (if prescribed by US vet)
      - Bring enough for full trip + 3 extra days
- [ ] **Obtain screwworm certificate** if travelling to/from an affected area
      (APHIS Form 7001 with screwworm statement; see Mexico requirements below)
- [ ] **Purchase pet travel kit**:
      - Collapsible water/food bowl
      - Small bag of regular food (avoid GI upset)
      - Portable litter box / pee pads
      - Leash + harness + muzzle (if required by airline)
      - Comfort blanket/toy

---

## 3 Days Before

- [ ] **Confirm all reservations** by phone or email:
      - Airline (pet reservation + seat assignment)
      - Hotel (pet policy, early check-in if needed)
      - Private transfer (pickup time, pet transport)
      - VetCancun (final procedure time)
- [ ] **Confirm screwworm certificate** is issued & signed (if applicable)
- [ ] **Re-check health certificate expiration** — must still be valid on departure day
- [ ] **Notify US veterinarian** of travel dates + expected return
- [ ] **Print / Save digital copies** of all documents:
      - Passport
      - Health certificate (APHIS-endorsed)
      - Vaccination records
      - Screwworm certificate
      - Airline itinerary & pet reservation confirmation
      - Hotel booking confirmation
      - VetCancun appointment confirmation

---

## Day Before

- [ ] **Pack pet travel kit** (see checklist above)
- [ ] **Pack human essentials** (medications, chargers, comfortable clothing)
- [ ] **Feed pet a light meal** in the evening (no food 4–6 hours before flight unless
      directed otherwise by vet)
- [ ] **Walk/exercise pet** to reduce stress before travel
- [ ] **Charge phone, camera, laptop** — you'll want photos for records
- [ ] **Set alarm** — arrive at FLL at least 3 hours before international departure

---

## Day Of — FLL Departure

- [ ] **Morning potty break** — give pet ample time to relieve themselves
- [ ] **Final health check** — pet is alert, hydrated, no vomiting/diarrhoea
- [ ] **Administer sedative ONLY if prescribed by vet** — never over-the-counter
- [ ] **Leave for FLL** with 3+ hours before departure
- [ ] **Check-in at airline counter**:
      - Present pet carrier for size check
      - Pay pet fee (if not prepaid)
      - Show health certificate if asked
      - Obtain boarding pass with pet annotation
- [ ] **Clear TSA security** — pet stays in carrier through X-ray (carrier goes through,
      you carry pet through metal detector)
- [ ] **Gate arrival** — keep pet calm, offer water, use potty area if available

---

## After Arrival — Cancun (CUN)

- [ ] **Immigration** — present passport; no pet-specific immigration form needed
- [ ] **Customs / SENASICA (agriculture inspection)**:
      - Present APHIS-endorsed health certificate
      - Present screwworm certificate (if applicable)
      - Dogs/cats may be subject to visual inspection by SENASICA officer
      - Pay any applicable inspection fees (typically none for personal pets)
- [ ] **Private transfer** to hotel (pre-booked; confirm driver at arrivals)
- [ ] **Check into hotel** — set up pet area, offer water, let pet rest
- [ ] **Visit VetCancun**:
      - Arrive at scheduled time with all records
      - Discuss procedure, sign consent forms
      - Review post-op care plan & medications
      - Confirm pick-up time
- [ ] **Post-op recovery** (if applicable):
      - Follow all VetCancun discharge instructions
      - Limit activity / use e-collar as directed
      - Monitor incision site for swelling / redness / discharge
- [ ] **Enjoy recovery downtime** — explore Cancun area while pet rests

---

## Return — CUN → FLL

- [ ] **Final vet check** at VetCancun before departure (if recommended)
- [ ] **Re-pack pet travel kit** (same as outbound)
- [ ] **Same feeding & exercise routine** as departure day
- [ ] **Arrive at CUN** 3 hours before international departure
- [ ] **Check in** — same airline pet procedure as FLL
- [ ] **SENASICA exit inspection** (rare for pets, but have documents ready)

---

## Return — Re-Entry to USA (FLL / CBP)

- [ ] **Present to U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP)**:
      - Passport for humans
      - CBP generally does NOT require a separate pet entry form for dogs/cats
        from Mexico
- [ ] **CDC import requirements** (dogs returning from Mexico):
      - **Mexico is NOT classified as high-risk for rabies** by the CDC
      - CDC Dog Import Form is NOT required for dogs returning from Mexico
        (as of current rules; check cdc.gov/importation/dogs for updates)
      - No rabies titer test needed
      - Vaccination records recommended but not mandatory for entry from Mexico
- [ ] **USDA/APHIS re-entry** — no separate APHIS inspection required
- [ ] **Declare any animal products** (food, treats) to CBP if carrying

---

## Post-Return — Follow-Up

- [ ] **Visit US veterinarian within 48–72 hours** of return for:
      - Post-op check (incision, pain, infection)
      - Remove sutures if non-dissolvable (or confirm VetCancun used dissolvable)
      - Update medical records with Cancun procedure details
- [ ] **Email records to MedPup** — send procedure summary, receipts, discharge
      instructions for MedPup's file
- [ ] **Submit client satisfaction feedback** to MedPup

---

## Mexico Import Requirements — Quick Reference

| Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Health Certificate** (CVP-70 / APHIS 7001) | Issued by USDA-accredited US vet; endorsed by APHIS. Valid 10 days from issue. |
| **APHIS Endorsement** | Must be stamped by USDA APHIS VS (allow 5–7 business days or use VSPS online). |
| **Rabies Vaccination** | Proof required; current vaccination (≥ 30 days prior for primary; no lapse for boosters). |
| **Microchip** | ISO 11784/11785 (15-digit); bring scanner-readable proof. |
| **Screwworm Certificate** | Required if travelling from screwworm-affected states. Statement on health certificate: *"Animals have been inspected and found free of screwworm (Cochliomyia hominivorax)."* |
| **SENASICA Inspection** | At-point-of-entry inspection by Mexican agricultural authorities. Typically visual only for personal pets. |

## CDC Rules — Dogs from Mexico (USA Re-Entry)

- Mexico is **NOT** a CDC high-risk rabies country — rules are relaxed.
- No CDC Dog Import Form required.
- No rabies titer (serology) test required.
- Dogs must appear healthy upon visual inspection.
- Always check [cdc.gov/importation/dogs](https://www.cdc.gov/importation/dogs/) for updates before each trip.
