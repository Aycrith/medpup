# MedPup — One Source of Truth
## Business Concept Feasibility Analysis
**Version:** 1.0 — May 18, 2026
**Methodology:** Primary-source verification via official government websites, CDC records, business registries, social media metadata, and commercial pet travel documentation. Where sources were blocked (Cloudflare, 403, JS-rendered content), this is explicitly noted.

---

## Executive Summary

This document systematically evaluates every key claim in the MedPup business concept against verifiable evidence. The analysis follows a strict verification-first methodology: claims are labeled CONFIRMED, CONTRADICTED, or UNVERIFIED. The concept as documented contains several material claims that are either contradicted by available evidence or cannot be verified from any public source.

**Bottom Line:** The core market thesis (US veterinary cost crisis, cross-border demand) is real and well-documented. However, the specific operational pathway — pets traveling on the Balearia ferry from Fort Lauderdale to Freeport for same-day veterinary procedures — rests on multiple unverified and potentially incorrect assumptions. The most critical path to feasibility is verifying (1) whether the ferry actually accepts pets, (2) whether a Freeport clinic can perform the claimed procedures, and (3) the actual pet import permit process and timeline.
